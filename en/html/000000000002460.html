Radiator and A/C Condenser Fan High Speed Circuit Troubleshooting
NOTE:
  • Do not use this troubleshooting procedure if only one fan is inoperative, or if the A/C compressor is inoperative. Refer to the symptom troubleshooting index.
  • Before doing any symptom troubleshooting, check for powertrain DTCs.
  • The normal operating A/C pressure for the radiator and A/C condenser fans to run at high speed is 1,770 kPa (18.5 kgf/cm 2 , 257 psi).
  1. Remove the A/C diode A.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between the No. 2 terminal of the A/C diode A 3P socket.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 4 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the fan control relay and the A/C diode A.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the A/C pressure switch 4P connector.
  1. Check for continuity between the No. 1 terminal of the A/C diode A 3P socket and the No. 2 terminal of the A/C pressure switch 4P connector.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 7 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the A/C diode A and the A/C pressure switch.■
  1. Disconnect ECM connector No. 2 (58P).
  1. Check for continuity between the No. 3 terminal of the A/C pressure switch 4P connector and the No. 39 terminal of ECM connector No. 2 (58P).
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 9 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the A/C pressure switch and the ECM.■
  1. Reinstall the A/C diode A.
  1. Connect the No. 2 and No. 3 terminals of the A/C pressure switch 4P connector with a jumper wire.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
Do the radiator and condenser fans run at high speed?
YES -
Replace the A/C pressure switch.■
NO -
Check for loose wires or poor connections at ECM connector No. 2 (58P). If the connections are good, substitute a known-good ECM, and recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away, replace the original ECM .■
