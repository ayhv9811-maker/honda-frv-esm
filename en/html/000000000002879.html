ATF Temperature Sensor Test and Replacement
  1. Raise the vehicle up on a lift, or apply the parking brake, block the rear wheels, and raise the front of the vehicle. Make sure it is securely supported.
  1. Remove the splash shield.
  1. Remove the drain plug (A), and drain the automatic transmission fluid (ATF).
  1. Reinstall the drain plug with the new sealing washer (B).
  1. Remove the shift solenoid valve cover (A), dowel pins (B), and gasket (C).
  1. Disconnect the shift solenoid harness connector (A), and remove it from the transmission housing. Remove the O-ring (B) from the connector.
  1. Measure ATF temperature sensor resistance between shift solenoid harness connector terminals No. 6 and No. 7.
    Standard:
    50 Ω−25 kΩ

  1. If the resistance is out of standard, go to Step 9 and replace the ATF temperature sensor-solenoid harness. The ATF temperature sensor is not available separately from the solenoid harness.
    If the resistance is within standard, install the shift solenoid harness connector with the new O-ring in the transmission housing, then go to Step 12 .
  1. Disconnect the connectors from the shift solenoid valve A, shift solenoid valve B, shift solenoid valve C, shift solenoid valve D, and shift solenoid valve E.
  1. Install a new O-ring (F) on the shift solenoid harness connector (G), and install the connector in the transmission housing.
  1. Connect the harness terminals to the solenoids:
    • RED wire connector to shift solenoid valve E.
    • GRN wire connector to shift solenoid valve C.
    • ORN wire connector to shift solenoid valve B.
    • BLU wire connector to shift solenoid valve A.
    • YEL, WHT, WHT wire connector to shift solenoid valve D.
  1. Install the shift solenoid valve cover, dowel pins, and a new gasket.
  1. Check the connector for rust, dirt, or oil, then connect the connector securely.
  1. Refill the transmission with ATF.
  1. Install the splash shield.
