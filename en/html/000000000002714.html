How to Troubleshoot the M/T Reverse Lockout System
How to Troubleshoot Circuits at the ECM
NOTE: The ECM overwrite data and monitors the EVAP system for up to 15 minutes after the ignition switch is turned OFF. Jumping the SCS line after turning the ignition switch OFF cancels this function. Disconnecting the ECM during this function, without jumping the SCS line first, can damage the ECM.
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Connect the HDS to the DLC (A).
    NOTE: If the HDS does not communicate with the ECM, troubleshoot the DLC circuit.
  1. Jump the SCS line with the HDS.
  1. Check for loose or poor connections at ECM connectors, and check for poor connections or loose terminals at the connectors in the circuit.
  1. Troubleshoot a problem on the reverse lockout system circuit with a digital multitester (A) and a pin probe (B). When troubleshooting at the ECM connector (C), slide a suitable pin probe into the inspection port (D) at the ECM connector terminal side. Do not slide the probe into the ECM connector terminals (E).
