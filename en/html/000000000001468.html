Headliner Removal/Installation
SRS components are located in this are. Review the SRS component locations and the precautions and procedures before performing repairs or service.
NOTE:
  • When prying components, use the handy remover set or equivalent.
  • Take care not to bend and scratch the headliner.
  • Be careful not to damage the dashboard and other interior trim.
  • RHD is shown; LHD is symmetrical.
  1. Remove these items:
    • Front side seats, both sides
    • Front pillar trim, both sides
    • Center pillar upper trim, both sides
    • Rear pillar trim, both sides
    • Kick panel, passenger's side
    • Front door panel, passenger's side
    • Front door speaker, passenger's side
    • Front individual map light
    • Ceiling light
    • Rear ceiling light
  1. Slide the rear center seat and both rear side seats all the way back.
  1. From both sides, remove the sunvisor (A) and holder (B).
    -1.
    Remove the sunvisor from the holder.
    -2.
    Insert a flat-tip screwdriver through the gap in the front side of the cap (C) and push the hook (D). While pushing the hook, rotate the sunvisor with sunvisor stay base (E) back 45 ° to remove the sunvisor from the body.
    -3.
    Using a flat-tip screwdriver, push the hook (F), and turn the holder 90 °, then pull it out.
  1. Pry out the lid (A), remove the self-tapping ET screw, then remove the center sunvisor (B).
  1. With ultrasonic sensor: Remove the roof console (A).
    -1.
    Remove the lenses (B).
    -2.
    Pry up on the front edge of the roof console trim (C) with a handy remover to release the hooks (D), then remove the cover.
    -3.
    Remove the bolts (E, F).
    -4.
    Pull out the roof console and front individual map light (G).
    -5.
    Disconnect the front individual map light connector (H), ultrasonic sensor connector (I), and if equipped, sunroof switch connector.
  1. Remove all of the grab handles (A).
    -1.
    Lower the grab handle then pry out the caps (B) by releasing the hooks (C) and tabs (D).
    -2.
    Pull out the stoppers (E).
    -3.
    Remove the grab handle from both clips (F).
  1. Using a pair of pliers, remove all of the clips (A) by pinching its hooks from the grab handle bracket (B).
  1. Lower the rear of the rear center seat belt retractor cover (A) to detach the clips, and pull the front hook (B) off the rear center seat belt retractor (C), then remove the cover.
  1. Lower the headliner (A).
    -1.
    Remove the front door opening seals (B), rear door opening seals (C), and tailgate weatherstrip (D) from each roof portion.
    -2.
    With the help of an assistant, detach the rear clips (E) by pulling the rear portion of headliner down, and lowering the headliner.
    -3.
    With sunroof: Release the fasteners (F) by pulling the front portion of the headliner down, and release the headliner from the sunroof frame (G) by sliding the headliner rearward.
    -4.
    Without sunroof: Remove the aluminium seal (H), then remove the interior wire harness (I) from the headliner.
Without sunroof
With sunroof
  1. Remove the headliner (A) through the passenger's door opening. RHD is shown; LHD is symmetrical.
    -1.
    While lowering the driver's side of the headliner, turn it counterclockwise (RHD) or clockwise (LHD).
    -2.
    Carefully move the headliner in the vehicle, then bring its front portion near to the passenger's door opening with its rear end out of the driver's side rear door opening.
    -3.
    Align the front driver's side corner of the headliner to the front lower corner of the front passenger's door opening, and align the opposite side corner of it to the rear upper corner of the opening.
    -4.
    Slightly bend both front curved portion (B) of the headliner until it can be removed out from the front passenger's door opening.
    -5.
    Pull the front of the headliner out from the opening with the rear of it apart from the center pillar, then remove the headliner.
  1. If necessary, remove the self-tapping ET screw, then remove the roof console bracket (A).
  1. Install the headliner in the reverse order of removal, and note these items:
    • If the side curtain airbag has been deployed, replace the headliner with new one.
    • To prevent the side curtain airbags from deploying improperly and possibly causing injury, inspect removed pieces and replace them if they have any of these types of damage:
      • -
      Any crease or tears in the headliner (A)
      -
      Any cracks or breakages in the grab handle (B)
      -
      Any damages around the grab handle holes (C) or sunvisor holes in the headliner
      -
      Any cracks in the sunvisor stay base (D)
      -
      Any bends or cracks in the sunvisor stay shaft (E)
      -
      Any cracks in the sunvisor base (F)
      -
      Any cracks or breakages in the vanity mirror base (G)
      -
      Any fastners (H) and clip bases (I) which have come off the headliner
    • When install the grab handle clip (J) to the grab handle bracket, make sure the upper and lower hooks of the clip snap into place securely
    • Make sure the top of the pillar trim overlaps with the headliner correctly.
    • Replace any damaged clips with new ones.
    • Replace the removed aluminium seals with new ones.
    • Check that both sides of the headliner are securely attached to the trim.
    • When reinstalling the headliner through the front passenger's door opening, be careful not to fold or bend it. Also, be careful not to scratch the body.
    • If the threads on a center sunvisor and roof console screws are worn out, use an oversized self-tapping ET screw made specifically for this application:
      Center sunvisor/roof console bracket:
      P/N 90136-S2H-003
    • Install the base portion of the sunvisor to the installation hole, then rotate the sunvisor toward the windshield until it locks. After installing, be sure the sunvisor doesn't come off the body when rotating it toward the door glass.
