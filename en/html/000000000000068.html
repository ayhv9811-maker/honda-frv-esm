VSA Activation Indicator Circuit Troubleshooting
VSA activation indicator does not come on
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II), and watch the VSA activation indicator.
Does the VSA activation indicator come on and go off?
YES -
The system is OK at this time.■
NO -
Go to Step 2 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the VSA control unit 46P connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Connect the VSA control unit 46P connector terminal No. 16 and body ground with a jumper wire.
Does the VSA activation indicator come on?
YES -
Check for loose or poor connections in the VSA control unit 46P connector. If connections are good, replace the VSA modulator-control unit .■
NO -
Go to Step 5 .
  1. Remove the gauge assembly.
  1. Disconnect the gauge assembly connector B (28P).
  1. Check for continuity between gauge assembly connector B (28P) terminal No. 12 and body ground.
Is there continuty?
YES -
Repair short to body ground in the wire between the gauge assembly and the VSA modulator-control unit.■
NO -
Replace the gauge assembly. ■
VSA activation indicator does not go off, and no DTCs are stored
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II), and watch the VSA activation indicator.
Does VSA activation indicator go off within 2 seconds?
YES -
The system is OK at this time.■
NO -
Go to Step 2 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the VSA control unit 46P connector.
  1. Check for continuity between VSA control unit 46P connector terminal No. 37 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between VSA modulator-control unit and body ground (G204).■

  1. Measure the voltage between VSA control unit 46P connector terminal No. 14 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 7 .
NO -
