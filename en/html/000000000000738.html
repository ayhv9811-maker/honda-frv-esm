Clutch Master Cylinder Removal
D17A2 engine LHD model
NOTE: Do not spill brake fluid on the vehicle; it may damage the paint; if brake fluid does contact the paint, wash it off immediately with water.
  1. Remove the brake fluid from the clutch master cylinder reservoir with a syringe.
  1. Write down the frequencies for the radio's preset buttons. Disconnect the negative (âˆ’) cable first, then the positive (+) cable from the battery. Remove the battery.
  1. Remove the air cleaner housing.
  1. Remove the clutch pipe brackets (A).
  1. Remove the clutch line (A) from the clutch line clips (B).
  1. Pry out the lock pin (A), and pull the pedal pin (B) out of the yoke. Remove the master cylinder mounting nuts (C).
  1. Remove the yoke (A) from the clutch master cylinder.
  1. Protect the brake master cylinder using a shop towel, then remove the clutch master cylinder (B).
  1. Disconnect the reservoir hose (A), then remove the retaining clip (B) and remove clutch pipe (C) from the clutch master cylinder (D). Plug the end of the reservoir hose and clutch pipe with a shop towel to prevent brake fluid from coming out.
  1. Remove the O-ring (E) and clutch master cylinder seal (F) from the clutch master cylinder.
