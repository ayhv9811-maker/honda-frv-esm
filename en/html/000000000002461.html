Radiator and A/C Condenser Fan Low Speed Circuit Troubleshooting
NOTE: Do not use this troubleshooting procedure if the A/C compressor is inoperative. Refer to the symptom troubleshooting index.
  1. Check the No. 10 (30 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box, and the No. 18 (7.5 A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box.
Are the fuses OK?
YES -
Go to Step 2 .
NO -
Replace the fuse(s), and recheck.■
  1. Remove the A/C condenser fan relay from the under-hood fuse/relay box, and test it.
Is the relay OK?
YES -
Go to Step 3 .
NO -
Replace the A/C condenser fan relay .■
  1. Measure the voltage between the No. 1 terminal of the A/C condenser fan relay 4P socket and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 4 .
NO -
Replace the under-hood fuse/relay box.■
  1. Connect the No. 1 and No. 2 terminals of the A/C condenser fan relay 4P socket with a jumper wire.
Do the A/C condenser and radiator fans run?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Go to Step 26 .
  1. Disconnect the jumper wire.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between the No. 3 terminal of the A/C condenser fan relay 4P socket and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 8 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the No. 18 fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box and the A/C condenser relay.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Reinstall the A/C condenser fan relay.
  1. Remove the A/C diode B.
  1. Using the diode setting ( ) on a DVOM, check for current flow in both directions between the No. 1 and No. 2 terminals of A/C diode B.
Is there current flow in only one direction?
YES -
Go to Step 12 .
NO -
Replace A/C diode B.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between the No. 2 terminal of the A/C diode B 2P socket and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 14 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the A/C condenser relay and the A/C diode B.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Reinstall the A/C diode B.
  1. Disconnect the A/C pressure switch 4P connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between the No. 4 terminal of the A/C pressure switch 4P connector and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 19 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the A/C diode B and the A/C pressure switch.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect climate control connector A (16P).
  1. Check for continuity between the No. 3 terminal of climate control unit connector A (16P) and the No. 1 terminal of the A/C pressure switch 4P connector.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 22 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the climate control unit and the A/C pressure switch.■
  1. Reconnect climate control connector A (16P).
  1. Connect the No. 1 and No. 4 terminals of the A/C pressure switch 4P connector with a jumper wire.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II), then push the A/C and fan control buttons ON.
Do the radiator and condenser fans run at high speed?
YES -
Go to Step 25 .
NO -
Replace the A/C pressure switch.■
  1. Substitute a known-good climate control unit, and recheck.
Is the symptom/indication goes away?
YES -
Replace the original climate control unit .■
NO -
Replace the climate control panel.■
  1. Disconnect the jumper wire.
  1. Reinstall the A/C condenser fan relay.
  1. Disconnect the A/C condenser fan 2P connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II), then push the A/C and fan control buttons ON.
  1. Measure the voltage between the No. 2 terminal of the A/C condenser fan 2P connector and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 31 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the A/C condenser fan relay and the A/C condenser fan.■
  1. Push the OFF button, then turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Reconnect the A/C condenser fan 2P connector.
  1. Connect the No. 1 terminal of the A/C condenser fan 2P connector to body ground with a jumper wire.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II), then push the A/C and fan control buttons ON.
Does the A/C condenser fan run?
YES -
Go to Step 35 .
NO -
Replace the A/C condenser fan motor.■
  1. Push the OFF button, then turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the jumper wire.
  1. Remove the fan control relay from the under-hood relay box, and test it.
Is the relay OK?
YES -
Go to Step 38 .
NO -
Replace the fan control relay.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II), then push the A/C and fan control buttons ON.
  1. Measure the voltage between the No. 1 terminal of the fan control relay 5P socket and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 40 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the A/C condenser fan and the fan control relay.■
  1. Push the OFF button, then turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the radiator fan 2P connector.
  1. Measure the voltage between the No. 2 terminal of the radiator fan 2P connector and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 43 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the fan control relay and the radiator fan high switch.■
  1. Check for continuity between the No. 1 terminal of the radiator fan 2P connector and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Replace the radiator fan motor.■
NO -
Check for an open in the wire between the radiator fan and body ground. If the wire is OK, check for poor ground at G301.■
