A/C Compressor Clutch Overhaul
  1. Remove the center nut (A) (D17A2 engine model) or center bolt (B) (K20A9 engine model) while holding the armature plate (D17A2 engine model) or pressure plate (K20A9 engine model) with the special tool (C).
D17A2 engine model
K20A9 engine model
  1. D17A2 engine model: Remove the armature plate (A) and shim(s) (B), taking care not to lose the shim(s). If the clutch needs adjustment, increase or decrease the number and thickness of shims as necessary, then reinstall the armature plate, and recheck its clearance.
    NOTE: The shims are available in four thicknesses: 0.1 mm, 0.2 mm, 0.4 mm and 0.5 mm.
  1. K20A9 engine model: Remove the pressure plate (A) and shim(s) (B), taking care not to lose the shim(s). If the clutch needs adjustment, increase or decrease the number and thickness of shims as necessary, then reinstall the pressure plate, and recheck its clearance.
    NOTE: The shims are available in three thicknesses: 0.1 m, 0.3 mm and 0.5 mm.
  1. If you are replacing the field coil, remove the snap ring (A) with snap ring pliers, then remove the pulley (B). Be careful not to damage the pulley or the A/C compressor.
D17A2 engine model
K20A9 engine model
  1. Remove the field coil.
    D17A2 engine model
    Remove the bolt and holder (A), then disconnect the field coil connector (B). Loosen the clamp screw (C) to free the field coil wire. Remove the snap ring (D) with snap ring pliers, then remove the field coil (E). Be careful not to damage the field coil and A/C compressor.
    K20A9 engine model
    Remove the screw from the field coil ground terminal (A), then remove the wire harness (B) and the bracket (C). Disconnect the field coil connector (D). Remove the snap ring (E) with snap ring pliers, then remove the field coil (F). Be careful not to damage the field coil or the A/C compressor.
  1. Reassemble the clutch in the reverse order of disassembly, and note these items:
    • Install the field coil with the wire side facing down, and align the boss on the field coil with the hole in the A/C compressor.
    • Clean the rotor pulley and A/C compressor sliding surfaces with contact cleaner or other non-petroleum solvent.
    • Install new snap rings, note the installation direction, and make sure they are fully seated in the groove.
    • Make sure that the rotor pulley turns smoothly after it's reassembled.
    • Route and clamp the wires properly or they can be damaged by the rotor pulley.
