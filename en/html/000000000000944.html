Engine Removal
NOTE:
  • Use fender covers to avoid damaging painted surfaces.
  • To avoid damage, unplug the wiring connectors carefully while holding the connector portion.
  • Mark all wiring and hoses to avoid misconnection. Also, be sure that they do not contact other wiring or hoses, or interfere with other parts.
  1. Secure the hood in the wide open position (support rod in the lower hole).
  1. Relieve fuel pressure.
  1. Remove the battery.
  1. Remove the harness clamp (A), then remove the resonator (B).
  1. Remove the air cleaner housing.
  1. Remove the throttle cable (A) by loosening the locknut (B), then slipping the cable end out of the accelerator linkage. Take care not to bend the cable when removing them. Always replace any kinked cable with a new one.
  1. Remove the battery cables from the under-hood fuse/relay box.
  1. Remove the harness clamp (A) and ground cables (B).
  1. Remove the two bolts (C), and loosen the two bolts (D) securing the battery base, then remove the battery base (E).
  1. Remove the quick-connect fitting cover (A), then disconnect the fuel feed hose.
  1. Remove the evaporative emission (EVAP) canister hose (B) and brake booster vacuum hose (C).
  1. Remove the passenger's dashboard lower cover.
  1. Disconnect the engine control module (ECM) connectors (A) and main wire harness connector (B), then remove the harness clamps (C).
RHD model:
LHD model:
  1. Remove the harness clamps (A) and grommet (B), then pull the engine wire harness through the bulkhead.
  1. Remove the power steering (P/S) pump belt.
  1. Remove the alternator-A/C compressor belt.
RHD model:
LHD model:
  1. Remove the 6 mm bolt (A) securing the adjust plate, then remove the locknut (B) and mounting nut (C).
  1. Remove the P/S pump, then remove the P/S hose from its clamp. Do not disconnect the P/S hoses from the pump.
  1. Remove the three bolts securing the shift cable holder, then remove the shift cable and select cable. Do not bend the cables excessively.
  1. Remove the clutch slave cylinder and clutch line bracket mounting bolt. Do not operate the clutch pedal once the slave cylinder has been removed.
  1. Remove the radiator cap.
  1. Raise the vehicle on the hoist to full height.
  1. Remove the front tyres/wheels.
  1. Remove the splash shield.
  1. Loosen the drain plug in the radiator, and drain the engine coolant.
  1. Drain the engine oil.
  1. Drain the transmission fluid.
  1. Disconnect the primary heated oxygen sensor (primary HO2S) connector (A) and secondary heated oxygen sensor (secondary HO2S) connector (B), then remove exhaust pipe A/three way catalytic converter (TWC) assembly (C).
  1. Separate the knuckles from the lower arms.
  1. Remove the driveshafts. Coat all precision-finished surfaces with clean engine oil. Tie plastic bags over the driveshaft ends.
  1. Remove the rear mount mounting bolts.
  1. Lower the vehicle on the hoist.
  1. Remove the upper radiator hose (A), lower radiator hose (B), and heater hoses (C).
  1. Remove the radiator.
  1. Remove the A/C compressor without disconnecting the A/C hoses.
  1. Attach the chain hoist to the engine/transmission as shown.
  1. Remove the front mount mounting bolt.
  1. Remove the upper bracket.
  1. Remove the resonator bracket (A), then remove the transmission mount bracket (B) and transmission mount (C).
  1. Check that the engine/transmission is completely free of vacuum hoses, fuel and coolant hoses, and electrical wiring.
  1. Slowly raise the engine/transmission about 150 mm (6 in.). Check once again that all hoses and electrical wiring are disconnected from the engine/transmission.
  1. Raise the engine/transmission assembly all the way, and remove it from the vehicle.
