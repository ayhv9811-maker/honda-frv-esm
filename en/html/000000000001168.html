Engine Oil Pan Installation
  1. Remove all of the old liquid gasket from the oil pan mating surfaces, bolts, and bolt holes.
  1. Clean and dry the oil pan mating surfaces.
  1. Apply liquid gasket, P/N 08C70-K0334M or 08C70-X0331S, evenly to the engine block mating surface of the oil pan and to the inner threads of the bolt holes.
    NOTE: Do not install the parts if 4 minutes or more have elapsed since applying liquid gasket. Instead, reapply liquid gasket after removing the old residue.
  1. Install the oil pan.
  1. Tighten the bolts in two or three steps. In the final step, tighten all bolts, in sequence, to 12 N·m (1.2 kgf·m, 8.7 lbf·ft). Wipe off the excess liquid gasket on the each side of crankshaft pulley and flywheel.
    NOTE:
    • Wait at least 30 minutes before filling the engine with oil.
    • Do not run the engine for at least 3 hours after installing the oil pan.
  1. Install the clutch cover.
  1. If the engine is still in the vehicle, do the following steps.
  1. Install the subframe. Align the reference lines on the subframe with the bolt head center, then tighten the bolts .
  1. Tighten the new rear mount mounting bolts
  1. Tighten the new front mount mounting bolt.
  1. Connect the suspension lower arm ball joints.
  1. Install three way catalytic converter (TWC) assembly. Use new gaskets and new self locking nuts.
  1. Connect the air fuel ratio (A/F) sensor connector and secondary heated oxygen sensor (secondary HO2S) connector, then install the harness to the clamps
  1. Install the splash shield.
  1. Install the front tyres/wheels.
  1. Lower the vehicle on the hoist.
  1. Install the air cleaner housing assembly, then install the breather hose and harness clamp. Connect the intake air temperature (IAT) sensor connector.
  1. Refill the engine with engine oil.
