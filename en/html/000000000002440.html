Radiator and Fan Replacement
NOTE:
  • Use fender covers to avoid damaging painted surfaces.
  • To avoid damaging the wires and terminals, unplug the wiring connectors carefully while holding the connector portion.
  • Mark all wiring and hoses to avoid misconnection. Also, be sure that they do not contact other wiring or hoses, or interfere with other parts.
  1. Drain the engine coolant.
  1. Remove the front grille cover.
  1. Disconnect the hood switch connector (A),then remove the A/C condenser stay/rubber(B), radiator upper mount bracket/cushion (C), hood latch assembly (D), left radiator upper mount bracket/cushion/rubber (E), and intercooler stay (F).
  1. Remove the bulkhead (A), then remove the harness clamps (B) and water bypass hose (C).
  1. Clean any dirt off the quick connector (A), radiator, and upper radiator hose.
  1. Pull out the lock (B) by hand, then wiggle the quick connector loose, and remove it from the radiator. Do not use any tools to remove the quick connector.
  1. Raise the vehicle on the hoist to full height.
  1. Remove the splash shield.
  1. Clean any dirt off the quick connector (A), radiator, and lower radiator hose.
  1. Pull out the lock (B) by hand, then wiggle the quick connector loose, and remove it from the radiator. Do not use any tools to remove the quick connector.
  1. Remove the harness clamps (A), then disconnect the radiator fan switch (Low) connector (B), radiator fan motor connector (C), remove A/C compressor clutch connector (D) and water bypass hose (E).
  1. Lower the vehicle on the hoist.
  1. Remove the harness clamps (A).
  1. Disconnect the A/C condenser fan motor connector (A) and A/C compressor clutch connector (B), then remove water bypass pipe (C) from the A/C condenser fan shroud.
  1. Remove the two bolts (D), and loosen the two bolts (E) securing the A/C condenser fan shroud.
  1. Remove the two bolts (F), and loosen the two bolts (G) securing the radiator fan shroud.
  1. Move the radiator fan shroud assembly (H) to left side of the vehicle, then pull up it.
  1. Move the A/C condenser fan shroud assembly (I) to left side of the vehicle, then pull up it.
  1. Pull up the radiator (A), then remove radiator fan switch (Low) (B) and drain plug (C).
  1. Install the radiator fan switch (Low) and drain plug with new O-rings (D), then install the radiator in the reverse order of removal. Make sure the lower cushions (E) are set securely.
  1. Install the radiator fan shroud, then install the A/C condenser fan shroud.
  1. Install the A/C compressor clutch connector and water bypass pipe on the A/C condenser fan shroud, then connect the A/C condenser fan motor connector and radiator fan motor connector.
  1. Check the quick connector (A) and set ring (B) for cracks or damage. If the connector and/or set ring are cracked or damaged, replace the connector.
  1. Make sure the set ring is in place inside the quick connector. If the set ring is off the connector, replace the quick connector.
  1. Replace the O-ring (C) in the quick connector.
  1. Check the lock (D). If the lock is damaged or deformed, replace it. When installing the new lock on the connector, press it straight down along the groove.
  1. Clean the connecting surfaces of the radiator (E), then apply clean engine coolant around the connecting surfaces.
  1. Push down the lock (A), then push the quick connector (B) onto the radiator until you hear it click.
  1. Push down the lock (A), then push the quick connector (B) onto the radiator until you hear it click.
  1. Install the A/C condenser brackets and radiator upper brackets/cushions.
  1. Install the bulkhead in the reverse order of removal. Apply touch-up paint to the bulkhead mounting bolts.
  1. Connect the hood latch switch connector, and install the harness clamps.
  1. Adjust the hood latch alignment.
  1. Install the splash shield.
  1. Install the front grille cover.
  1. Refill the expansion tank with engine coolant, and bleed air from the cooling system with the heater valve open.
