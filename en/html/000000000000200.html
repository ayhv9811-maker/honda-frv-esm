Front Door Latch Replacement
NOTE: Put on gloves to protect your hands.
Latch Replacement - KG Model
  1. Remove these items:
    • Door panel
    • Panel bracket
  1. Disconnect the power door lock actuator connector.
  1. Remove the plastic cover.
  1. Raise the glass fully.
  1. Disconnect the outer handle rod from the outer handle.
  1. Pull the retainer out, then disconnect the cylinder cable from the latch.
  1. Detach the latch cable (A) and inner handle cable (B) from the holder (C).
  1. Remove the bolt (D) and screws (E) securing the latch (F), then remove the latch through the hole in the door. Take care not to bend the outere handle rod (G), lock cable, and inner handle cable.
  1. Install the latch in the reverse order of removal, and note these items:
    • Make sure the actuator connector is plugged in properly and each rod is connected securely.
    • Make sure the door locks and opens properly.
    • When reinstalling the door panel, make sure the plastic cover is installed properly and sealed around its outside perimeter to seal out water.
Latch Replacement - KE Model
  1. Remove these items:
    • Door panel
    • Panel bracket
  1. Disconnect the power door lock actuator connector.
  1. Remove the plastic cover.
  1. Raise the glass fully.
  1. Disconnect the outer handle rod from the outer handle.
  1. Remove the outer handle protector mounting bolt, then release the protector from the outer handle.
  1. Detach the latch cable (A) and inner handle cable (B) from the holder (C).
  1. Remove the bolt (D) and screws (E) securing the latch (F), then remove the latch through the hole in the door. Take care not to bend the outer handle rod (G), cylinder cable (H), latch cable, and inner handle cable.
  1. Driver's: While pressing a middle flange portion (A) of the outer casing cover (B) against the latch, pull both outside flanges (C) of the retainer (D) upward.
  1. Driver's: Pull the middle flange portion (A) of the outer casing cover (B) up, then disconnect the cylinder cable (C) from the latch.
    NOTE: As soon as the cable comes off the latch, the shutter (D) goes down into the connector (E).
  1. If necessary, remove the special screw, then separate the door cylinder (A) and outer handle protector (B). If the retainer (C) is damaged, release the hooks (D), and replace it.
  1. Install the latch in the reverse order of removal, and note these items:
    • Make sure the actuator connector is plugged in properly and each rod is connected securely.
    • Make sure the door locks and opens properly.
    • When reinstalling the door panel, make sure the plastic cover is installed properly and sealed around its outside perimeter to seal out water.
    • Cylinder cable installation: Before installing the cylinder cable (A) to the latch, hang the elbow of the shutter (B) on the boss (C) as shown. Next install the cylinder cable fully into the connector (D) while slightly pulling the shutter back, then push the retainer (E) until it snaps to engage the latch and the door cylinder securely. Make sure the elbow of the shutter is on the boss.
Cable Replacement
  1. Remove the latch.
  1. Remove the lever cover (A).
    -1.
    Remove the screw.
    -2.
    Release the hooks (B) from the latch body (C).
    -3.
    Remove the lever cover from under the latch (D) with its forward side away from the body. Take care not to damage the connector seal (E) with the cover.
  1. Disconnect the latch cable (A).
  1. Disconnect the inner handle cable (A).
  1. Install the cable in the reverse order of removal, and note these items:
    • Make sure each cable is connected securely.
    • Make sure the door latches and unlatches properly.
    • While installing the lever cover, take care not to damage the connector seal.
