Parking Brake Check and Adjustment
Check
NOTE:
  • To apply the parking brake, just pull the brake lever (A) without pushing the release button (B).
  • To release the parking brake, push and hold the release button, pull the brake lever, and then return the brake lever smoothly.
  • To apply the parking brake again, return the brake lever all the way back.
  1. Pull the parking brake lever (A) with 196 N (20 kgf, 44 lbf) of force to fully apply the parking brake. The parking brake lever should be locked within the specified number of clicks (B).
    Lever locked clicks:
    5 to 7

  1. Adjust the parking brake if the lever clicks are not within the specification.
Minor Adjustment
  1. Block the front wheels, then raise the rear of the vehicle, and support it with safety stands in the proper location.
  1. Slide the left seat all the way back, then lift up the floor carpet (A) at the insulator cutout.
    NOTE: Use universal wrench to reach the adjusting nut (B) to adjust its notches.
  1. Pull the parking brake lever up one click.
  1. Tighten the adjusting nut unit the parking brakes drag slightly when the rear wheels are turned.
    NOTE: The adjusting nut is the self-lock nut. If the nut does not turn smoothly, check the adjusting bolt (C). Do not turn the adjusting nut forcefully. It may damage the parking brake cable and adjusting bolt.
  1. Release the parking brake lever fully, and check that the parking brakes do not drag when the rear wheels are turned. Readjust if necessary.
  1. Make sure the parking brakes are fully applied when the parking brake lever is pulled all the way (5 to 7 clicks).
Major Adjustment (to be done when replacing parking brake shoes and after lining surface break-in)
  1. Raise the rear of the vehicle, and support it with safety stands in the proper locations.
  1. Release the parking brake lever fully.
  1. Back off the adjusting nut (A) in the equalizer.
  1. Remove the rear wheels.
  1. Remove the access plug (A).
  1. Turn the ratchet teeth (B) on the adjuster assembly (C) with a flat-tip screwdriver (D) until the shoes lock against the parking brake drum. Then back off the adjuster 8 clicks, and install the access plug.
  1. Do the minor adjustment procedure.
  1. Install the rear wheels.
