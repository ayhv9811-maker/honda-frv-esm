Rear Knuckle/Hub Bearing Unit Replacement

Exploded View
Hub Bearing Unit Replacement
  1. Raise the rear of the vehicle, and support it with safety stands in the proper locations.
  1. Remove the rear wheel.
  1. Rlease the parking brake lever.
  1. Remove the brake hose mounting bracket (A).
  1. Remove the caliper bracket mounting bolts (B), and hang the caliper (C) to one side. To prevent damage to the caliper or brake hose, use a short piece of wire to hang the caliper from the undercarriage. Do not twist the brake hose with force.
  1. Remove the hub cap (A), raise the stake (B), and remove the spindle nut (C).
  1. Remove the brake disc retaining screws (A).
  1. Screw 8 x 1.25 mm bolts (B) into the disc (C) to push it away from the hub. Turn each bolt two turns at a time to prevent cocking the disc excessively.
  1. Remove the brake disc/drum.
  1. Remove the hub bearing unit (A) from the spindle.
  1. Install the hub bearing unit in the reverse order of removal, and note these items:
    • Tighten all mounting hardware to the specified torque values.
    • Before installing the brake disc/drum, clean the mating surface of the hub bearing unit and the brake disc/drum.
    • Use a new spindle nut on reassembly.
    • After tightening the spindle nut, use a drift to stake the spindle nut shoulder against the spindle.
    • Use a new hub cap on reassembly.
    • Before installing the wheel, clean the mating surface of the brake disc/drum and the inside of the wheel.
Knuckle Replacement
  1. Remove the hub bearing unit.
  1. Remove the parking brake assembly (A). To prevent damage to the backing plate or parking brake shoes assembly and cable, use a short piece of wire to hang the backing plate from the undercarriage. Do not twist the parking brake cable with force.
  1. Remove the wheel sensor (A), brake hose mounting bracket (B) from the knuckle. Do not disconnect the wheel sensor connector.
  1. Place the floor jack under the trailing arm (A), and support the suspension.
    NOTE: Do not place the jack against the plate section of the trailing arm.
  1. Remove the flange bolt (B), and disconnect the upper arm (C) from the knuckle.
  1. Mark the cam positions of the adjusting bolt (A) and adjusting cam (B), then remove the self-locking nut (C), adjusting cam, and adjusting bolt. Discard the self-locking nut.
  1. Remove the flange bolt (D), and remove the knuckle (E).
  1. Install the knuckle in the reverse order of removal, and note these items:
    • First install all the suspension components, and lightly tighten bolts and nuts, then place a floor jack under the trailing arm, and raise the suspension to load it with the vehicle's weight before fully tightening bolts and nuts to the specified torque values.
    • Tighten all the mounting hardware to the specified torque values.
    • Align the cam positions of the adjusting bolt and adjusting cam with the marked positions when tightening.
    • Use a new self-locking nut on reassembly.
    • Use a new spindle nut on reassembly.
    • After tightening the spindle nut, use a drift to stake the spindle nut shoulder against the spindle.
    • Before installing the brake disc/drum, clean the mating surface of the hub bearing unit and the inside of the brake disc/drum.
    • Use a new hub cap on reassembly.
    • Before installing the wheel, clean the mating surface of the brake disc/drum and the inside of the wheel.
    • Check the wheel alignment, and adjust it if necessary.
