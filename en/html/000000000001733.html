DTC Troubleshooting: P2645 (21)
DTC P2645:
 VTEC System Malfunction
Special Tools Required

Oil pressure gauge attachment
 07NAJ-P070100

Low pressure gauge
 07406-0070002

Oil pressure hose
 07ZAJ-S5A0200
  1. Reset the ECM.
  1. Check the engine oil level, and refill if necessary. Check for external damage to the oil pan.
  1. Start the engine. Hold the engine speed at 3,000 rpm (min −1 ) without load (in neutral) until the radiator fan comes on.
  1. Road test the vehicle:
    Accelerate in 2nd gear to an engine speed over 4,000 rpm (min −1 ). Hold that engine speed for at least 2 seconds. If DTC P2645 is not repeated during the first road test, repeat this test 2 more times.
Is DTC P2645 indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Intermittent failure, system is OK at this time. Check the oil consumption if oil was added in Step 2 . Check for poor connections or loose terminals at the rocker arm oil control solenoid (VTEC solenoid valve) and the ECM.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the rocker arm oil control solenoid (VTEC solenoid valve) 1P connector.
  1. Check for resistance between the rocker arm oil control solenoid (VTEC solenoid valve) 1P connector terminal and body ground.
Is there 14−30 Ω?
YES -
Go to Step 8 .
NO -
Go to Step 20 .
  1. Disconnect ECM connector B (24P).
  1. Check for continuity between ECM connector terminal B15 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between the rocker arm oil control solenoid (VTEC solenoid valve) and the ECM (B15), then go to Step 21 .
NO -
Go to Step 10 .
  1. Connect the rocker arm oil control solenoid (VTEC solenoid valve) 1P connector terminal to body ground with a jumper wire.
  1. Check for continuity between ECM connector terminal B15 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 12 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the rocker arm oil control solenoid (VTEC solenoid valve) and the ECM (B15), then go to Step 21 .
  1. Remove the rocker arm oil pressure switch (VTEC oil pressure switch) (A), and install the special tools as shown, then install the rocker arm oil pressure switch (VTEC oil pressure switch) in the pressure gauge attachment (B).
    NOTE: Install the switch in the reverse order of removal with a new O-ring.
  1. Reconnect ECM connector B (24P), the rocker arm oil control solenoid (VTEC solenoid valve) 1P connector, and the rocker arm oil pressure switch (VTEC oil pressure switch) 2P connector.
  1. Start the engine. Hold the engine speed at 3,000 rpm (min −1 ) without load (in neutral) until the radiator fan comes on.
  1. Check oil pressure at engine speeds of 1,000 and 2,000 rpm (min −1 ). Keep measuring time as short as possible because the engine is running without load (less than 1 minute).
Is pressure below 49 kPa (0.5 kgf/cm 2 , 7 psi)?
YES -
Go to Step 16 .
NO -
Inspect the rocker arm oil control solenoid (VTEC solenoid valve). ■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the rocker arm oil control solenoid (VTEC solenoid valve) 1P connector.
  1. Attach the battery positive terminal to the rocker arm oil control solenoid (VTEC solenoid valve) 1P connector terminal.
  1. Start the engine, then connect the battery negative to body ground and check oil pressure at an engine speed of 3,000 rpm (min −1 ).
Is pressure above 390 kPa (4.0 kgf/cm 2 , 57 psi)?
YES -
Go to Step 27
NO -
Inspect the rocker arm oil control solenoid (VTEC solenoid valve). ■
  1. Replace the rocker arm oil control solenoid (VTEC solenoid valve).
  1. Reconnect all connectors.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Reset the ECM with the HDS.
  1. Do the ECM idle learn procedure.
  1. Do the VTEC TEST in the INSPECTION MENU with the HDS.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Are any Temporary DTCs or DTCs indicated?
YES -
If DTC P2646 is indicated, check for poor connections or loose terminals at the rocker arm oil control solenoid (VTEC solenoid valve) and the ECM, then go to Step 1 . If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.
NO -
Troubleshooting is complete.■
  1. Update the ECM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good ECM.
  1. Do the VTEC TEST in the INSPECTION MENU with the HDS.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Are any Temporary DTCs or DTCs indicated?
YES -
If DTC P2646 is indicated, check for poor connections or loose terminals at the rocker arm oil control solenoid (VTEC solenoid valve) and the ECM, then go to Step 1 . If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.
NO -
If the ECM was updated, troubleshooting is complete. If the ECM was subsutituted, replace the original ECM. ■
