DTC Troubleshooting: J
DTC indicator J:
 A Short in the Air Mix Control Motor Circuit
  1. Clear the DTC by turning the ignition switch OFF, and then ON (II).
  1. Operate the climate control system in several modes.
  1. Check for DTCs using the self-diagnostic.
Is DTC J indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 4 .
NO -
Intermittent failure.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Test the air mix control motor.
Is the air mix control motor OK?
YES -
Go to Step 6 .
NO -
Replace the air mix control motor. ■
  1. Disconnect the air mix control motor 5P connector.
  1. Disconnect climate control unit connector B (20P).
  1. Check for continuity between the No. 1, 2, 5, 15, and 16 terminals of climate control unit connector B (20P) and body ground individually.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair any short to body ground in the wire(s) between the climate control unit and the air mix control motor.■
NO -
Go to Step 9 .
  1. Check for continuity between the terminals of climate control unit connector B (20P) as follows.
    Terminal
    To terminals
    B1
    B2, B5, B15, B16
    B2
    B5, B15, B16
    B5
    B15, B16
    B15
    B16

Is there continuity between any of the terminals?
YES -
Repair the short in the wires.■
NO -
Go to Step 10 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II), and check the same terminals for voltage.
Is there any voltage?
YES -
Repair any short to power in the wire(s) between the climate control unit and the air mix control motor. This short may also damage the climate control unit. Repair the short to power before replacing the climate control unit .■
NO -
Go to Step 11 .
  1. Substitute a known-good climate control unit, and recheck.
Is the symptom/indication goes away?
YES -
Replace the original climate control unit .■
NO -
Replace the climate control panel.■
