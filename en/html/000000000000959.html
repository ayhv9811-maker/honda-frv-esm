Piston, Pin, and Connecting Rod Replacement
Special Tools Required

Piston pin assembly tool set
 07PAF-0010000

Pilot collar, O.D. 19 mm
 07PAF-0010650
Disassembly
  1. Remove the piston from the engine block.
  1. Set the pilot collar (A) over the piston pin assembly tool set (B), and adjust the piston pin assembly tool set as shown, then tighten the screws (C). Remove the pilot collar.
  1. With the embossed mark facing up, place the piston assembly (A) on the special tool (B). Be sure you position the recessed flat on the piston against the lugs on the special tool.
  1. Press the pin (C) out with the driver (D), pilot collar (E), and a hydraulic press.
Inspection
  1. Measure the diameter of the piston pin.

    Piston Pin Diameter

    Standard (New):
    18.996−19.000 mm (0.7479−0.7480 in.)

  1. Zero the dial indicator to the piston pin diameter.
  1. Measure the piston pin-to-piston clearance.

    Piston Pin-to-Piston Clearance

    Standard (New):
    0.010−0.020 mm (0.0004−0.0008 in.)

  1. Check the difference between the piston pin diameter and connecting rod small end diameter.

    Piston Pin-to-Connecting Rod Interference

    Standard (New):
    0.016−0.036 mm (0.0006−0.0014 in.)

Reassembly
  1. Assemble the piston and connecting rod with the connecting rod oil hole (A) facing the ‘‘IN'' mark (B) side.
  1. Insert the pilot collar (A) into the piston and connecting rod.
  1. Adjust the length of the pin driver (B) to 47 mm (1.9 in.)
  1. With the embossed mark facing up, place the piston assembly (C) on the special tool (D). Be sure you position the recessed flat on the piston against the lugs on the base attachment.
  1. Press the pin (E) in with the pin driver and a hydraulic press.
