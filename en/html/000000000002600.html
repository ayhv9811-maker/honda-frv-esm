A/C Condenser Fan High Speed Circuit Troubleshooting
NOTE:
  • Do not use this troubleshooting procedure if the radiator fan and/or the A/C compressor is inoperative. Refer to the symptom troubleshooting index.
  • Before performing symptom troubleshooting, check for powertrain DTCs.
  1. Check the No. 18 (7.5 A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box.
Is the fuse OK?
YES -
Go to Step 2 .
NO -
Replace the fuse, and recheck.■
  1. Remove the fan control relay from the under-hood relay box, and test it.
Is the relay OK?
YES -
Go to Step 3 .
NO -
Replace the fan control relay.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between the No. 3 terminal of the fan control relay 5P socket and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the No. 18 fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box and the fan control relay.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Check for continuity between the No. 2 terminal of the fan control relay 5P socket and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 7 .
NO -
Check for an open in the wire between the fan control relay and body ground. If the wire is OK, check for poor ground at G301.■
  1. Disconnect the radiator fan high switch 2P connector.
  1. Check for continuity between the No. 5 terminal of the fan control relay 5P socket and the No. 1 terminal of the radiator fan high switch 2P connector.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 9 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the fan control relay and the radiator fan high switch.■
  1. Check for continuity between the No. 2 terminal of the radiator fan high switch 2P connector and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Replace the radiator fan high switch.■
NO -
Check for an open in the wire between the radiator fan high switch and body ground. If the wire is OK, check for poor ground at G301.■
