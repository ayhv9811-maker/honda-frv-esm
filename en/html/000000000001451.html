Steering Gearbox Installation
NOTE: RHD type is shown, LHD type is symmetrical.
  1. Pass the valve body unit side of the steering gearbox (A) together with the tie-rods (B) through the wheel well opening on the driver's side.
  1. Carefully move the steering gearbox toward the driver's side until the pinion shaft clears the heater hoses.
  1. Install the pinion shaft grommet (A).
  1. Pull the steering gearbox into the engine compartment while turning the pinion shaft (B), and move the steering gearbox in the direction of the front passenger's seat side.
    NOTE: Take care not to deform the boots while pulling the steering gearbox into the engine compartment.
  1. Pass the pinion shaft into the cabin. Install the driver's seat side gearbox mounting bracket (A) noting the installation direction, and loosely tighten the mounting bolts (B).
    NOTE: Check that the grommet is set in position securely and check for foreign material being caught in the area where the parts are in close contact with each other.
  1. Install the mounting cushion (A) on the cylinder side of the steering gearbox with the gap (B) of the mounting cushion in the position shown.
    NOTE: Install the mounting cushion on the steering gearbox during the work of Step 2 to facilitate operation of this step.
  1. Install the front passenger's seat side gearbox mounting bracket (C) on the cushion noting the installation direction. Loosely tighten the nuts (D).
  1. Tight the loosely tightened steering gearbox mounting bolts and mounting nuts to the specified torque.
  1. Loosely tighten the flare nut of the feed pipe (A) and that of the return pipe joint (B) against the valve body unit.
  1. Vehicle with D17A2 engine model LHD type, K20A9 engine model RHD type: Install the feed pipe clamp (A) and return pipe clamp (B).
  1. Vehicle with D17A2 engine model RHD type, K20A9 engine model LHD type: Install the feed pipe bracket (A) and return pipe clamp (B).
  1. Tighten the flare nut of the feed pipe and that of the return pipe to the specified torque.
Front passenger's seat side
Driver's seat side
  1. Set the steering rack in the straight ahead position and connect the right and left tie-rod ends (A) to the corresponding damper (B).
    NOTE: When the tie-rod end is loosened, tighten the locknut (C) during adjustment of the front toe.
  1. Set a new cotter pin (D) on the ball joint pin and bend the pin ends as shown with (E) or (F) of the illustration.
  1. Check that the front wheels and steering rack are in the straight ahead position.
  1. Insert the yoke joint into the pinion shaft with the groove (A) of the yoke joint at the angle shown.
    NOTE: Do not turn the pinion shaft not to move the steering rack out of the straight ahead position.
  1. Align the slot (A) in the pinion shaft with the bolt hole (B) in the yoke joint, and tighten the joint bolt (C) to the specified torque.
  1. Pull the yoke joint toward the column and be sure that it does not come off the pinion shaft.
  1. Install the steering wheel.
  1. Reinstall the driver's dashboard cover.
  1. Install the front wheels.
  1. Fill the system with power steering fluid, and bleed air from the system.
  1. After installation, do the following checks:
    • Start the engine, allow it to idle, and turn the steering wheel from lock-to-lock several times to warm up to the fluid. Check the gearbox for leaks.
    • Do the front toe inspection.
    • Check the steering wheel spoke angle. Adjust by turning the right and left tie-rods equally if necessary.
