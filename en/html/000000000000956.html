Crankshaft Installation
Special Tools Required

Handle driver
 07749-0010000

Oil seal driver attachment, 96 mm
 07ZAD-PNA0100
  1. Check the connecting rod bearing clearance with plastigage.
  1. Check the main bearing clearance with plastigage.
  1. Install the bearing halves in the engine block and connecting rods.
  1. Apply a coat of engine oil to the main bearings and rod bearings.
  1. Hold the crankshaft so rod journal No. 2 and rod journal No. 3 are straight up, and lower the crankshaft into the block.
  1. Install the thrust washers (A) in the No. 4 journal of the engine block.
  1. Seat the rod journals into connecting rod No.1 and connecting rod No. 4. Line up the mark (B) on the connecting rod and cap, then install the caps and nuts finger-tight.
  1. Rotate the crankshaft clockwise, and seat the journals into connecting rod No. 2 and connecting rod No. 3. Line up the mark on the connecting rod and cap, then install the caps and nuts finger-tight.
  1. Tighten the connecting rod nuts to 32 N·m (3.3 kgf·m, 24lbf·ft).
  1. Put the bearing cap bridge on the engine block.
  1. Apply engine oil to the threads of the bearing cap bolts.
  1. Tighten the bearing cap bolts in sequentially in two steps.
    1st Step Torque:
    25 N·m (2.5 kgf·m, 18 lbf·ft)
    2nd Step Torque:
    51 N·m (5.2 kgf·m, 38 lbf·ft)

  1. The seal mating surface on the engine block end cover should be dry.
  1. Drive the crankshaft oil seal squarely into the engine block end cover.
  1. Confirm that the clearance is equal all the way around with a feeler gauge.
    Clearance:
    0.5−0.8 mm (0.02−0.03 in.)

  1. Apply a light coat of grease to the crankshaft and to the lip of the seal.
  1. Remove all of the old liquid gasket from the engine block end cover mating surfaces, bolts, and bolt holes.
  1. Clean and dry the engine block end cover mating surfaces.
  1. Apply liquid gasket, P/N 08C70-K0234M, 08C70- K0334M, or 08C70-X0331S, evenly to the engine block mating surface of the engine block end cover and to the inner threads of the bolt holes.
    NOTE: Do not install the parts if 4 minutes or more have elapsed since applying liquid gasket. Instead, reapply liquid gasket after removing old residue.
  1. Install the dowel pins (A), and the engine block end cover (B) on the engine block.
  1. The seal mating surface on the oil pump should be dry.
  1. Drive the crankshaft oil seal squarely into the oil pump.
  1. Apply a light coat of grease to the crankshaft and to the lip of the seal.
  1. Remove all of the old liquid gasket from the oil pump mating surfaces, bolts, and bolt holes.
  1. Clean and dry the oil pump mating surfaces.
  1. Apply liquid gasket, P/N 08C70-K0234M, 08C70- K0334M, or 08C70-X0331S, evenly to the engine block mating surface of the oil pump and to the inner threads of the bolt holes.
    NOTE: Do not install the parts if 4 minutes or more have elapsed since applying liquid gasket. Instead, reapply liquid gasket after removing old residue.
  1. Apply oil to the new O-ring (A).
  1. Install the dowel pins (B), then align the inner rotor with the crankshaft and install the oil pump (C).
  1. Install the oil screen (D) with a new gasket (E).
  1. Clean the excess grease off the crankshaft and check the seal for distortion.
  1. Install the oil pan.
  1. Install the cylinder head.
  1. Install the flywheel, then install the clutch disc and pressure plate.
  1. Install the transmission.
  1. Install the engine assembly.
    NOTE: Whenever any crankshaft or connecting rod bearing is replaced, it is necessary after reassembly to run the engine at idling speed until it reaches normal operating temperature, then continue to run it for about 15 minutes.
