DTC Troubleshooting: P2646 (22)
DTC P2646:
 VTEC Oil Pressure Switch Circuit Low Voltage
Special Tools Required

Pressure gauge attachment
 07NAJ-P070100

Low pressure gauge
 07406-0070002

Oil pressure hose
 07ZAJ-S5A0200
  1. Check the engine oil level.
Is the level OK?
YES -
Go to Step 2 .
NO -
Adjust the engine oil to the proper level, then go to Step 26 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS.
  1. Do the VTEC TEST in the INSPECTION MENU with the HDS.
Is the result OK?
YES -
Intermittent failure, system is OK at this time. Check for poor connections or loose terminals at the VTEC oil pressure switch and the ECM.■
NO -
Go to Step 5 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the VTEC oil pressure switch 2P connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Check the VTEC PRES SW in the DATA LIST with the HDS.
Is SWITCH ON indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 22 .
NO -
Go to Step 9 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Remove the VTEC oil pressure switch (A), and install the special tools as shown, then install the VTEC oil pressure switch in the pressure gauge attachment (B).
    NOTE: Install the switch in the reverse order of removal with a new O-ring.
  1. Reconnect the VTEC oil pressure switch 2P connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Do the VTEC TEST in the INSPECTION MENU with the HDS.
  1. Check the oil pressure.
Does the oil pressure increase to at least 392 kPa (4.0 kgf/cm 2 , 56.9 psi)?
YES -
Replace the VTEC oil pressure switch, then go to Step 25 .
NO -
Go to Step 15 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect VTEC solenoid valve 1P connector.
  1. Measure resistance between VTEC solenoid valve 1P connector terminal and solenoid valve body.
Is there 14−30 Ω at room temperature?
YES -
Go to Step 18 .
NO -
Replace the VTEC solenoid valve, then go to Step 25 .
  1. Disconnect ECM connector B (24P).
  1. Check for continuity between ECM connector terminal B15 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between the ECM (B15) and the VTEC solenoid valve, then go to Step 25 .
NO -
Go to Step 20 .
  1. Connect the VTEC solenoid valve 1P connector terminal and body ground with a jumper wire.
  1. Check for continuity between ECM connector terminal B15 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 31 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the ECM (B15) and the VTEC solenoid valve, then go to Step 25 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect ECM connector B (24P).
  1. Check for continuity between ECM connector terminal B9 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between the ECM (B9) and the VTEC oil pressure switch, then go to Step 25 .
NO -
Go to Step 31 .
  1. Reconnect all connectors.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Reset the ECM with the HDS.
  1. Do the ECM idle learn procedure.
  1. Do the VTEC TEST in the INSPECTION MENU with the HDS.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Are any Temporary DTCs or DTCs indicated?
YES -
If DTC P2646 is indicated, check for poor connections or loose terminals at the VTEC oil pressure switch and the ECM, then go to Step 1 . If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.
NO -
Troubleshooting is complete.■
  1. Update the ECM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good ECM.
  1. Do the VTEC TEST in the INSPECTION MENU with the HDS.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Are any Temporary DTCs or DTCs indicated?
YES -
If DTC P2646 is indicated, check for poor connections or loose terminals at the VTEC oil pressure switch and the ECM, then go to Step 1 . If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.
NO -
If the ECM was updated, troubleshooting is complete. If the ECM was subsutituted, replace the original ECM. ■
