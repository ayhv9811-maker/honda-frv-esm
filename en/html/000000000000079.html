Steering Column Removal and Installation
SRS components are located in this area. Review the SRS component locations and the precautions and procedures before performing repairs or service.
Removal
  1. Disconnect the negative cable from the battery.
  1. Remove the driver's airbag assembly and the steering wheel.
  1. Remove the driver's dashboard under cover.
  1. To prevent the spline shaft (A) from dropping during removal of the steering column, secure the spline shaft against the spline tube (B) using a wire (C).
  1. Do not route the wire at the bearing (D) of the joint to secure the spline shaft. Be sure to route the wire between the yokes (E) of the spline shaft.
  1. Release the hooks (A) of the upper column cover by pushing the upper column cover (B) from the front side.
  1. Release the hook (C) locating on the left side of the lower column cover (D) from the pin hole (E) in the lower column cover.
  1. Insert a suitable sized screwdriver or equivalent tool along the guide rib (F) into the pin hole and release the hook.
  1. Release the remaining hooks of the column cover and remove the upper column cover.
  1. Remove the lower column conver (A).
  1. Remove the steering joint bolt (B), then disconnect the steering joint (C) from the pinion shaft.
  1. Disconnect the connectors from the ignition switch, and release the wire harness clips (D) from the steering column.
  1. Disconnect the wire harness connectors from the combination switch assembly (E).
  1. Remove the combination switch assembly from the steering column shaft by remobing the screws (F).
  1. Remove the steering column (G) by removing the attaching nuts and bolts. If the lower slide shaft (H) is removed, slip it into the upper shaft (I) by aligning the marks (J).
  1. Remove the centre guide (K) (if equipped) from the top of the pinion shaft, and discard it.
Installation
  1. Install the steering column in the reverse order of removal, and note these items:
    • Take care not to let the sliding plates fall out of position during column installation.
    • Make sure the wires are not caught or pinched by any parts.
    • Cut the wire.
  1. Check that the front wheels and steering rack are in the straight ahead position.
  1. Insert the yoke joint into the pinion shaft with the groove (A) of the yoke joint at the angle shown.
    NOTE: Do not turn the pinion shaft not to move the steering rack out of the straight ahead position.
  1. Align the slot (A) in the pinion shaft with the bolt hole (B) in the yoke joint, and tighten the joint bolt (C) to the specified torque.
  1. Pull the yoke joint toward the column and be sure that it does not come off the pinion shaft.
  1. Finish the installation, and note these items:
    • Make sure the connectors are properly connected.
    • Reinstall the steering wheel.
    • Reconnect the negative cable to the battery.
    • Enter the customer's presets, and set the clock.
    • Verify cruise control, audio remote, and turn signal switch operation.
    • Make sure the steering wheel is centred.
    • Reinstall the driver's seat side under cover.
