Clutch Master Cylinder Installation
D17A2 engine LHD model
NOTE: Do not spill brake fluid on the vehicle; it may damage the paint; if brake fluid does contact the paint, wash it off immediately with water.
  1. Install the new O-ring (A) and a clutch master cylinder seal (B).
  1. Fit to the clutch master cylinder (C), the reservoir hose (D), the clutch pipe (E), and a new retaining clip (F).
    When installing the clutch pipe to the clutch master cylinder, apply 0.05−0.1 g (0.0018−0.0035 oz) of the brake assembly lube on the mating surface.
  1. Make sure the hose clamps (A) are positioned on the master cylinder (B) and reservoir (C) as shown.
  1. To prevent the retaining clip (A) from coming off, pry to open the tip of the retaining clip (B) with a screwdriver.
  1. Protect the brake master cylinder using a shop towel, then install the clutch master cylinder (A).
  1. Install the yoke (B) to the clutch master cylinder.
  1. Tighten the master cylinder mounting nuts (A).
  1. Install the pedal pin (B) and the lock pin (C).
  1. Install the clutch line (A) onto the clutch line clips (B).
  1. Install the clutch pipe brackets (A).
  1. Install the air cleaner housing.
  1. Install the battery, then connect the battery cables.
  1. Bleed the clutch master hydraulic system.
    NOTE: The reservoir filling is covered in the bleeding procedure.
  1. Adjust the clutch pedal and clutch pedal position switch.
