Headlight Leveling Motor Input Test - Without HID
Without HID
NOTE: Before testing, check for a blown No. 9 (7.5 A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box.
  1. Disconnect the 3P connector (A) from the headlight leveling motor (B).
  1. Check for continuity between the No. 3 terminal and body ground. There should be continuity.
    • If there is no continuity, check for:
      • -
      An open in the BLK wire between the headlight leveling motor and body ground.
      -
      A poor ground (G201, G302).
    • If there is continuity, go to Step 3 .
  1. Check for voltage between the No. 1 terminal and body ground. There should be battery voltage.
    • If there is no battery voltage, check for:
      • -
      An open in the YEL or BRN wire between the headlight leveling motor and under-dash fuse/relay box.
    • If there is battery voltage, go to Step 4 .
  1. Using an ohmmeter, measure resistance between the No. 2 terminal and body ground in position 0 of the headlight leveling switch. There is about 4.0 kâ„¦.
    • If the resistance is not as specified, check for:
      • -
      A poor ground (G201, G302).
      -
      An open in the GRN wire between the headlight leveling motor and headlight leveling switch.
      -
      A faulty headlight leveling switch.
      -
      An open in the BLK wire between the headlight leveling motor and body ground.
    • If the resistance is as specified, go to Step 5 .
  1. If all tests are normal, but the headlight leveling motor does not work, check for frozen, stuck or improperly installed headlight leveling motor. If the mechanical check is OK, replace the headlight leveling motor and housing assembly.
  1. After installing, recheck the system.
