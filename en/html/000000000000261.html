Dashboard/Steering Hanger Beam Removal/Installation
SRS components are located in this area. Review the SRS component locations and the precautions and procedures before performing repairs or service.
NOTE:
  • When prying components, use the handy remover set or equivalent.
  • Have an assistant help you when removing and installing the dashboard.
  • Take care not to scratch the dashboard, body and other related parts.
  • Put on gloves to protect your hands.
  1. Disconnect the negative cable from the battery, and wait at least 3 minutes before beginning work.
  1. Remove these items:
    • Driver's dashboard lower cover
    • Driver's under cover
    • Dashboard center lower cover
    • Center lower panel
    • Glove box
    • Front pillar trim, both sides
    • Steering column
  1. Disconnect the shift cable.
Driver's side
  1. From the under-dash fuse/relay box (A) and from under the dash, disconnect the main wire harness connectors (B), floor wire harness connectors (C), interior wire harness connectors (D), right side wire harness connectors (E), right engine compartment wire harness connectors (F), and air hose (G). Release the air bose from the clip (H), and release the wire harness clips (I).
LHD
RHD
Middle portion
  1. Pull back the carpet (A) as needed, then remove the rear heater joint duct (B).
    -1.
    Remove the bolts (C) and release the clip (D).
    -2.
    Disconnect the floor wire harness connector (E), and detach the connector clip (F).
    -3.
    Move the duct against the heater unit to disconnect it from the rear heater duct (G).
    -4.
    Remove the duct from the heater unit.
    NOTE: RHD is shown; LHD is symmetrical.
  1. Disconnect the SRS control unit connectors (A), parking brake switch connector (B) yaw rate-lateral acceleration sensor connector (C), radio antenna feeder connector (D), and if equipped, GPS antenna connector (E), then release the wire harness clips (F). Using a T30 Torx bit, remove the ground bolt (G).
LHD
RHD
  1. From under the dash, disconnect the ECM connector (A), power transistor connector (B), engine wire harness connector (C), A/C subharness connector (D), and throttle actuator control module connector (E), then release the wire harness clips (F).
LHD
RHD
Passenger's side
  1. From under the dash, disconnect the right engine compartment wire harness connectors (A), main wire harness connector (B), floor wire harness connector (C) (with navigation system) and right side wire harness connector (D) (LHD).
  1. Detach all of the harness and connector clips.
LHD
RHD
  1. Remove bolts, then remove the brake pedal support member (A).
  1. Remove the special bolts (A) from outside the passenger's door. RHD is shown, LHD is symmetrical:
    -1.
    Remove the caps (B).
    -2.
    Loosen the special bolt until its threads have come off the sleeve (C). The bolt will be screwed into the inside female screw of the adjusting nut (D) by loosening the bolt. Because of the thread locks on the thread of the bolt, the bolt and adjusting nut will be fixed.
    -3.
    Loosen the bolt again to screw the outside left-hand male screw of the adjusting nut into the sleeve fully, then the gap (E) will be between the nut and body.
    -4.
    Remove the bolt.
  1. If the adjusting (A) nut didn't be screwed into the sleeve fully while removing the special bolts, screw its left-hand male screw into the sleeve with 21 mm open-end wrench:
    • In this case, this bolt should be replaced with new one because its thread locks were worn out.
    • RHD is shown; LHD is symmetrical except the direction of screwing.
  1. From outside the driver's door, remove the caps (A), then remove the bolts (B, C, D). RHD is shown; LHD is symmetrical.
  1. Lift up on the dashboard (E) to release it from the guide pins (F). Carefully remove the dashboard through the front door opening. Take care not to scratch the body with the adjusting nuts on passenger's side.
  1. Remove the bolts, then remove the floor joint bracket (A) from the center frame (B).
  1. Install the dashboard in the reverse order of removal, and note these items:
    • Make sure the dashboard fits onto the guide pins correctly.
    • Before tightening the bolts, make sure each wire harness is not pinched.
    • Make sure the connectors are plugged in properly, and the antenna lead and each cable are connected properly.
    • If you screwed the adjusting nut with an open end wrench to remove the dashboard, because any of the special bolts came off the adjusting nut before having screwed the adjusting nut into the sleeve fully, replace the special bolt as new one.
    • Before reinstalling the dashboard, screw all of the adjusting nuts into the sleeves fully by hand, reinstall the floor joint bracket on the center frame, and slightly tighten the mounting bolts.
    • After setting the dashboard on the body, reinstall all of the mounting bolts to the dashboard, tighten the driver's side bracket with these tightening torque, then torque the special bolt (A) on passenger's side.
      • -
      First screw the special bolt (A) into the adjusting nut (B), then they will be fixed because of the thread lock on the bolt. Go on tightening the bolt with the adjusting nut, the outside left-hand mail screw of the nut is loosen from the sleeve until the nut contacts the inside face of the body. Then tighten the bolts again with its tightening torque fully.
      -
      If the adjusting nut doesn't contact the body while tightening the special bolt fully, the nut shouldn't be loosen with an open-end wrench. In this case, replace this special bolt as new one because it is wrong.
    • After tightening the dashboard mounting bolts, tighten the center bracket mounting bolts and center frame mounting bolts.
    • Reconnect the negative cable to the battery.
    • Set the clock.
    • Perform the power window control unit reset procedure. And on D17A engine model, do the ECM idle learn procedure.
    • Check for any DTCs that may have been set during repairs, and clear them.
