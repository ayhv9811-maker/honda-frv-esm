DTC Troubleshooting: 66
DTC 66:
 VSA Pressure Sensor (Software)
  1. On the HDS, select ABS TCS VSA, then DATA LIST. Check BRAKE SWITCH.
Is the brake switch OFF with the brake pedal pressed?
YES -
Go to Step 2 .
NO -
Go to Step 8 .
  1. Check the No. 14 (15 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box.
Is the fuse OK?
YES -
Reinstall the fuse, and go to Step 3 .
NO -
Replace the fuse.■
  1. Check the No. 20 (7.5 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box.
Is the fuse OK?
YES -
Reinstall the fuse, and go to Step 4 .
NO -
Replace the fuse.■
  1. Check the brake pedal position switch.
Is the switch OK and its adjustment OK?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Replace or adjust the brake pedal position switch.■
  1. Measure the voltage between brake pedal position switch 4P connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 6 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the No. 14 (15 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box and the brake pedal position switch.■
  1. Disconnect the VSA control unit 46P connector.
  1. Measure the voltage between body ground and the VSA control unit 46P connector terminal No. 6.
Is there battery voltage when the brake pedal is pressed?
YES -
Go to Step 8 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the brake pedal position switch and the VSA modulator-control unit.■
  1. Clear the DTCs using the HDS.
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF, then turn it ON (II), and press the brake pedal several times.
Does the ABS indicator come on, and is DTC 66 indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 10 .
NO -
The system is OK at this time.■
  1. On the HDS, select ABS TCS VSA, then DATA LIST. Check BRK PRESS.
Does BRK PRESS increase with the brake pedal pressed and decrease with the brake pedal released?
YES -
Replace the VSA modulator-control unit. ■
NO -
Check the brake lines for damage and leakage.■
