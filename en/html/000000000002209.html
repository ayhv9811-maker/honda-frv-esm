Engine Removal
NOTE:
  • Use fender covers to avoid damaging painted surfaces.
  • To avoid damaging the wires and terminals, unplug the wiring connectors carefully while holding the connector portion.
  • Mark all wiring and hoses to avoid misconnection. Also, be sure that they do not contact other wiring or hoses, or interfere with other parts.
  1. Secure the hood in the wide open position (support rod in the lower hole).
  1. Make sure you have the anti-theft codes for the radio and navigation system, then write down the customer's radio station presets.
  1. Disconnect the negative cable from the battery first, then disconnect the positive cable.
  1. Remove the engine cover.
  1. Remove the battery.
  1. Remove the air cleaner housing.
  1. Remove the battery base.
  1. Remove the intake stay (A), then remove the air intake duct (B).
  1. Remove the clutch line bracket (A).
  1. Remove the two bolts (B), and loosen the two bolts (C) securing the battery base bracket, then remove the battery base bracket (D).
  1. Remove the under-hood fuse/relay box cover.
  1. Remove the battery cables (A) from the under-hood fuse/relay box.
  1. Disconnect the harness connector (B).
  1. Remove the clutch slave cylinder, and clutch line bracket mounting nut.
  1. Remove the shift cable and select cable.
  1. Remove the intercooler outlet hose.
  1. Remove the fuel feed hose from the fuel pump.
  1. Remove the fuel return hose (A) and brake booster vacuum hose (B).
  1. Remove the passenger's under cover.
  1. Disconnect the engine control module (ECM) connectors (A), then remove the harness clamps (B).

LHD type:
  1. Remove the harness clamp (A), then disconnect the main wire harness connectors (B).
RHD type:
LHD type:
  1. Remove the harness clamps (A) and grommet (B), then pull the engine wire harness through the lower dashboard.
RHD type:
LHD type:
  1. Remove the expansion tank cap.
  1. Raise the vehicle on the hoist to full height.
  1. Remove the front wheels.
  1. Remove the splash shield (A) and engine under cover (B).
  1. Loosen the drain plug in the radiator, and drain the engine coolant.
  1. Drain the transmission fluid.
  1. Drain the engine oil.
  1. Disconnect the stabilizer links.
  1. Separate the knuckles from the lower arms.
  1. Remove the driveshafts. Coat all precision-finished surfaces with clean engine oil. Tie plastic bags over the driveshaft ends.
  1. Remove exhaust pipe A.
  1. Loosen the bands (A) securing the turbocharger outlet hose and intercooler intake hose, and remove the intercooler intake hose (B), then remove the intercooler intake pipe mounting bolts (C).
  1. Remove the intercooler intake pipe bracket (D), then remove the intercooler intake pipe (E).
  1. Lower the vehicle on the hoist.
  1. Remove the radiator.
  1. Remove the upper radiator hose.
  1. Remove the lower radiator hose.
  1. Remove the intercooler.
  1. Remove the harness clamp (A) and water bypass hoses (B), then remove the expansion tank (C).
  1. Remove the drive belt.
  1. Remove the power steering (P/S) pump (A), and the P/S hose clamp (B). Do not disconnect the P/S hoses from the pump.
  1. Remove the heater hoses.
  1. Remove the A/C compressor without disconnecting the A/C hoses.
  1. Attach the chain hoist using the sling belt and karabiner to the engine/transmission as shown.
    • Take care not to damage the A/C pipes.
    • The sling belt must use the one that the load of 1,000 kg (2,204 lbs) or more can be endure.
    • The karabiner must use the one that the load of 500 kg (1,102 lbs) or more can be endure.
  1. Raise the vehicle on the hoist.
  1. Remove the lower torque rod mounting bolt (A).
  1. Remove the two bolts securing the transmission lower mount.
  1. Make the appropriate reference lines at positions A and B that line up with the center of the subframe mounting bolts (C).
  1. Remove the five subframe mounting bolts, then lower the subframe.
  1. Remove the transmission upper mount bracket.
  1. Remove the ground cable (A), then remove the transmission upper mount (B).
  1. Remove the ground cable (A), then remove the upper torque rod (B).
  1. Remove the side engine mount bracket.
  1. Check that the engine/transmission is completely free of vacuum hoses, fuel and coolant hoses, and electrical wiring.
  1. Slowly lower the engine approximately 150 mm (6 in.). Check once again that all hoses and wires are disconnected from the engine/transmission.
  1. Lower the engine/transmission all the way. Remove the chain hoist from the engine/transmission.
  1. Remove the engine/transmission from under the vehicle.
