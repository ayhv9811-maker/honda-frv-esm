Rear Center Seat Disassembly/Reassembly - Without Leather
NOTE:
  • Take care not to tear the seams or damage the seat covers.
  • Put on gloves to protect your hands.
  • Use the handy remover set, or equivalent when prying components.
  1. Remove the rear center seat.
  1. Lift the seat cushion (A) up, and disconnect the slide cables (B) from both seat tracks (C).
  1. Remove the screws, and release the clips (A), then remove the arm bracket cover outers (B) from both arm brackets (C).
  1. Remove the bolts, then remove the seat cushion (A). If necessary, remove the bushings (B) from both arm brackets.
  1. Remove the seat cushion cover/pad from the seat cushion frame.
  1. Detach the clips (A), then remove the arm bracket cover inner (B) from the arm bracket (C). Remove the screw (D), then remove the buckle holder (E).
  1. Remove the seat-back cover/pad from the seat-back frame.
  1. Release the clips and hooks (A), then remove the recline cover outers (B) from both recline adjusters (C).
  1. Release the clips, then remove the recline cover inners (A) from both recline adjusters (B).
  1. Disconnect the recline control cable from both recline adjusters.
  1. Remove the bolts, then remove the seat-back frame (A).
  1. Remove the rear center seat belt buckle and rear center seat belt detachable anchor.
  1. Remove the screws (A), bolts (B, C), and nuts (D), then separate the upper seat track cover (E), connecting pipe (F), recline adjusters (G), and seat tracks (H).
  1. Reassemble the seat in the reverse order of disassembly, and note these items:
    • Apply multipurpose grease to the moving portions of both recline adjusters and both seat tracks.
    • Replace any damaged clips.
    • Push the clips and hooks into place securely.
    • Make sure each cable is connected securely.
    • Replace the bushing on each arm bracket if it's damaged.
    • Apply liquid thread lock to the buckle holder mounting screw before reinstallation.
