DTC Troubleshooting: 91
DTC 91:
 F-CAN Communication
  1. Check the PGM-FI DTCs.
Are any DTCs indicated?
YES -
Troubleshoot the PGM-FI DTCs, then recheck.■
NO -
Go to Step 2 .
  1. Connect the HDS to the DLC.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (ll).
  1. Check YAW RATE S and LATERAL ACCELERATION SENSOR in the DATA LIST with the HDS.
Do YAW RATE S and LATERAL ACCELERATION SENSOR show correct value?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Go to Step 7 .
  1. Clear the DTCs using the HDS.
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF, then turn it ON (ll).
Does the VSA indicator come on, and is DTC 91 indicated?
YES -
Check for loose or poor connections terminals in the VSA control unit 46P connector. If connections are good, replace the VSA modulator-control unit. ■
NO -
Intermittent failure. The system is OK at this time.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the yaw rate/lateral acceleration sensor 4P connector.
  1. Check for continuity between yaw rate/lateral acceleration sensor 4P connector No. 2 and No. 3.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 10 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the yaw rate/lateral acceleration sensor and the VSA modulator-control unit.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (ll).
  1. Measure the voltage between yaw rate/lateral acceleration sensor 4P connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 12 .
NO -
Repair open or high resistance in the wire between No. 3 (7.5 A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box and the yaw rate/lateral acceleration sensor.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Check for continuity between yaw rate/lateral acceleration sensor 4P connector terminal No. 4 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Replace the yaw rate/lateral acceleration sensor. ■
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the yaw rate/lateral acceleration sensor and the VSA modulator-control unit.■
