DTC Troubleshooting: P1030 (183)
DTC P1030:
 Fuel Heater Circuit Malfunction
NOTE: Before you troubleshoot, record all freeze data and review general troubleshooting information.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS.
  1. Do the FUEL HEATER ON/OFF in the INSPECTION MENU with the HDS.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P1030 indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Intermittent failure, the system is OK at this time. Check for poor connections or loose terminals at the fuel heater relay and the ECM.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Check the No. 18 FUEL HEATER MONITOR (7.5 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box.
Is the fuse OK?
YES -
Go to Step 7 .
NO -
Go to Step 11 .
  1. Check the No. 19 FUEL HEATER (30 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box.
Is the fuse OK?
YES -
Go to Step 8 .
NO -
Repair short in the wire between the fuel heater and the No. 19 FUEL HEATER (30 A) fuse. Also replace the No. 19 FUEL HEATER (30 A) fuse, then go to Step 16 .
  1. Check the No. 12 +B FUEL HEATER (40 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box.
Is the fuse OK?
YES -
Go to Step 9 .
NO -
Repair short in the wire between the No. 12 +B FUEL HEATER (40 A) fuse and the fuel heater relay. Also replace the No. 12 +B FUEL HEATER (40 A) fuse, then go to Step 16 .
  1. Disconnect the fuel heater relay.
  1. Check the fuel heater relay.
Is the fuel heater relay OK?
YES -
Go to Step 11 .
NO -
Replace the fuel heater relay, then go to Step 15 .
  1. Measure voltage between fuel heater relay 4P connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 12 .
NO -
Repair open or short in the wire between the No. 12 +B FUEL HEATER (40 A) fuse and the fuel heater relay, then go to Step 15 .
  1. Disconnect the fuel heater 2P connector.
  1. Disconnect ECM connector No. 1 (96P).
  1. Check for continuity between ECM connector No. 1 terminal No. 37 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between No. 18 FUEL HEATER MONITOR (7.5 A) fuse and ECM connector No. 1 terminal No. 37. If the No. 18 FUEL HEATER MONITOR (7.5 A) fuse was blown, replace it. Then go to Step 15 .
NO -
Go to Step 20 .
  1. Reconnect all connectors.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Reset the ECM with the HDS.
  1. Do the FUEL HEATER ON/OFF in the INSPECTION MENU with the HDS.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P1030 indicated?
YES -
Check for poor connections or loose terminals at the fuel heater, the fuel heater relay, and the ECM, then go to Step 1 .
NO -
Troubleshooting is complete. If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.■
  1. Reconnect all connectors.
  1. Update the ECM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good ECM.
  1. Do the FUEL HEATER ON/OFF in the INSPECTION MENU with the HDS.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P1030 indicated?
YES -
Check for poor connections or loose terminals at the fuel heater, the fuel heater relay, and the ECM. If the ECM was updated, substitute a known-good ECM, then go to Step 22 . If the ECM was substituted, go to Step 1 .
NO -
If the ECM was updated, troubleshooting is complete. If the ECM was substituted, replace the original ECM. If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.■
