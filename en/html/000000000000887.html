Alternator-A/C Compressor Belt Inspection and Adjustment
Special Tools Required

Belt tension gauge
 07JGG-0010101
Belt Tension Gauge Method
Inspection
  1. Remove the splash shield.
  1. Attach the belt tension gauge to the belt and measure the tension. Follow the gauge manufacturer's instructions. If the belt is worn or damaged, replace it. If the belt needs adjustment, go to Step 3 .
    Tension
    Used Belt:
    390−540 N (40−55 kgf, 88−120 lbf)
    New Belt:
    830−980 N (85−100 kgf, 190−220 lbf)

Adjustment
  1. Loosen the lock bolt (A) and mounting bolt (B).
  1. Torque the mounting bolt to 20 N·m (2.0 kgf·m, 14 lbf·ft). Turn the adjusting bolt (C) to obtain the proper belt tension, then retighten the lock bolt and mounting bolt to specified torque.
  1. Recheck the belt tension.
  1. If you installed a new belt, run the engine for 5 minutes, then readjust the belt to the used belt specification.
Deflection Method
Inspection
  1. Apply a force of 98 N (10 kgf, 22 lbf), and measure the deflection at the mid point between the alternator and crankshaft pulley. If the belt is worn or damaged, replace it. If the belt needs adjustment, go to Step 2 .
    Deflection
    Used Belt:
    8.5−11.0 mm (0.33−0.43 in.)
    New Belt:
    5.0−6.5 mm (0.20−0.26 in.)

Adjustment
  1. Loosen the lock bolt (A) and mounting bolt (B).
  1. Torque the mounting bolt to 20 N·m (2.0 kgf·m, 14 lbf·ft). Turn the adjusting bolt (C) to obtain the proper belt tension, then retighten the lock bolt and mounting bolt to specified torque.
  1. Recheck the belt tension.
  1. If you installed a new belt, run the engine for 5 minutes, then readjust the belt to the used belt specification.
