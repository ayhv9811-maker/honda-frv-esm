Coolant Temperature Gauge Troubleshooting
Before testing, check the No. 23 (10 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box and the No. 9 (7.5 A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box.
  1. Start the engine, and check the malfunction indicator lamp (MIL).
Does the MIL come on and stay on?
YES -
Check for PGM-FI system DTCs with the HDS, and go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting, then go back to Step 1 .
NO -
Go to Step 2 .
  1. Do the gauge drive circuit check with the self-diagnostic function.
Does the temperature gauge needle sweep from the minimum position to the maximum, then return to the minimum position?
YES -
Go to Step 3 .
NO -
Replace the gauge assembly.■
  1. Disconnect the gauge assembly connector B (28P). Disconnect the ECM connector A (31P). Check for continuity between the gauge assembly connector B (28P) No. 23 terminal and ECM connector A (31P) No. 13 *1 terminal or No. 24 *2 terminal.
    *1
    K20A9 engine
    *2
    D17A2 engine

Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 4 .
NO -
Open in the wire BRN wire between the gauge assembly connector B (28P) and ECM connector A (31P).■
  1. Substitute a known-good ECM, and recheck.
Did the symptom/indication go away?
YES -
Replace the ECM .■
NO -
Substitute a known-good gauge assembly. If the symptom/indication goes away, replace the gauge assembly.■
