  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).

YES -

NO -
Check for loose or poor connections at the connector. If the connection is OK, repair open or short in the wire between No. 8 (7.5 A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box and the reverse lockout solenoid.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Remove the reverse lockout solenoid.
  1. Connect a jumper wire from the negative battery terminal to the reverse lockout solenoid connector terminal No. 1, and connect another jumper wire from the positive battery terminal to the connector terminal No. 2. Make sure the reverse lockout solenoid works properly.
Does the reverse lockout solenoid work properly?
YES -
Go to Step 16 .
NO -
Replace the reverse lockout solenoid. ■
  1. Install the reverse lockout solenoid on the transmission, and connect the reverse lockout solenoid connector.
  1. Jump the SCS line with the HDS.
  1. Disconnect ECM connector A (44P).
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between ECM connector terminal A27 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Check for loose or poor connection at ECM connector terminal A27. If the connection is OK, update the ECM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good ECM, then recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away with a known-good ECM, replace the original ECM. ■
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the reverse lockout solenoid and the ECM.■
