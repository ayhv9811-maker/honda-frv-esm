Intake Manifold Removal and Installation

Exploded View
Removal
  1. Relieve fuel pressure.
  1. Remove the air cleaner housing.
  1. Remove the throttle cable (A) by loosening the locknut (B), then slipping the cable end out of the accelerator linkage. Take care not to bend the cable when removing them. Always replace any kinked cable with a new one.
  1. Remove the engine wire harness connectors and wire harness clamps from the intake manifold.
    • Manifold absolute pressure (MAP) sensor connector
    • Throttle position sensor connector
    • Idle air control (IAC) valve connector
    • Evaporative emission (EVAP) canister purge valve connector.
  1. Remove the EVAP canister hose (A), brake booster vacuum hose (B), positive crankcase ventilation (PCV) hose (C), and purge joint (D).
  1. Remove the water bypass hoses (A), then plug the water bypass hoses.
  1. Remove the hose clamp bracket (B).
  1. Remove the four fuel injector.
  1. Remove the injector base stiffener.
  1. Remove the harness cover (A), dipstick (B), and throttle cable clamps (C).
  1. Remove the intake manifold.
Installation
  1. Install the intake manifold (A) with a new gasket (B), and tighten the bolts/nuts in a crisscross pattern in two or three steps, beginning with the inner nut.
  1. Install the throttle cable clamps (A), dipstick (B), and harness cover (C).
  1. Install the injector base stiffener.
  1. Install the four injectors.
  1. Install the hose clamp bracket (A).
  1. Install the water bypass hoses (B).
  1. Install the purge joint (A), then install the EVAP canister hose (B), brake booster vacuum hose (C), and PCV hose (D).
  1. Install the throttle cable, then adjust the cable.
  1. Install the air cleaner housing.
  1. Refill the radiator with engine coolant, and bleed air from the cooling system with the heater valve open.
