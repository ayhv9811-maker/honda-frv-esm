Front Center Seat Disassembly/Reassembly
NOTE:
  • Take care not to tear the seams or damage the seat covers.
  • Put on gloves to protect your hands.
  • Use the handy remover set, or equivalent when prying components.
Seat-back
  1. Remove the front center seat.
  1. Reverse the turn over cushion.
  1. Remove the right recline cover (A).
    -1.
    Remove the screw (B), then remove the front slide knob (C).
    -2.
    Pull the cover back to detach the clips (D) and hooks (E).
    -3.
    Pull the cover up to release the hook (F).
    -4.
    Pull the recline strap (G) in through a slot in the cover.
  1. Remove the left recline cover (A).
    -1.
    Pull the cover back to detach the clips and front hooks (B).
    -2.
    Release the rear hooks (C).
    -3.
    Pull the cover up to release the hook (D).
  1. Remove the cushion rear trim (A).
    -1.
    Pull the upper portion of the trim back to detach the clips from the rear trim wires (B).
    -2.
    Lower the trim to release cover hole from the rear slide knob (C).
    -3.
    Slip the front center seat belt (D) out through the slit (E) in the trim.
  1. Detach the clips by pulling the upper portion of the table board A (A), then lift it up to release the lower hook (B), then remove it.
  1. Pull out the headrest guides (A) while pinching the end of the guides, and remove them.
  1. Remove shoulder cover upper (A) by detaching the clip and releasing the tab (B).
  1. Remove the front center seat belt upper anchor bolt (A).
  1. Release the clips fastening the shoulder cover upper (A), pull out the cover from the seat-back frame, and slip the front center seat belt (B) out through a slit (C) in the cover, then remove it.
  1. Fold the seat-back forward.
  1. Remove the screws and release the hooks (A), then remove the bottom trim (B) from the bottom of the seat-back, and slip the front center seat belt (C) out through a slit (D) in the trim.
  1. Remove the lower anchor bolt (A).
  1. Unzip the bottom of the seat-back cover (A), then release the clip and hooks (B).
  1. Release the trim cord (A) of the seat-back cover/pad (B) at the shoulder anchor portion from the hooks (C) of the webbing protector (D). Life the seat-back cover/pad to release them from the seat-back frame, and pull the front center seat belt (E) off the seat-back cover/pad through a hole in them. After removing the cover/pad, wrap the seat-back frame near the seat belt with a clean shop towel to prevent damage it during disassembly.
  1. Reverse the turn over cushion (A), then remove the cushion tray (B).
  1. Remove the screws, then remove the table board B (A).
  1. Release the clip fastening the top of the webbing protector (A), and release the middle hook (B).
  1. Lift the webbing protector (A) to release the bottom hooks (B) from the bracket of the seat-back frame, and remove the protector from the frame by lowering it.
  1. Remove the webbing protector (A) from the front center seat belt (B).
  1. Release all of the hook strips (A), then remove the seat cushion cover/pad (B) from the seat cushion frame (C). Pull the front center seat belt (D) and seat belt buckle (E) off the seat cushion cover through holes in the cover.
  1. Reassemble the seat-back/seat cushion in the reverse order of disassembly, and note these items:
    • To avoid damaging the seat belt during reassembly, wrap the seat-back frame near the seat belt with a clean shop towel.
    • To prevent wrinkles when installing a seat-back cover and seat cushion cover, make sure the material is stretched evenly over the pad before securing the hook and clips.
    • Apply liquid thread lock to the anchor bolts before reinstallation.
    • Before installing the webbing protector to the seat belt, make sure there are no twists or kinks in the seat belt. And before installing the anchor bolts, make sure there are no twists or kinks in the seat belt.
    • Replace any damaged clips with new ones.
    • Push the clips into place securely.
Seat-back Frame/Seat Cushion Frame/Seat Tracks
NOTE:
  • Apply multipurpose grease to the moving portion of the seat tracks.
  • Replace any damaged clips.
  • Push the clips into place securely.
