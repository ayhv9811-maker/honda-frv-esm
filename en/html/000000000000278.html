Interior Trim Removal/Installation - Center Pillar
Center Pillar Upper/Lower
  1. Remove these items:
    • Front door sill inner trim
    • Rear door sill inner trim
  1. Release both upper hooks (A) by pulling the both upper edges of the center pillar lower trim (B) out, then pull out along both sides to release the trim from the front and rear door opening seals (C).
  1. Remove the right center pillar lower trim (A) or left center pillar lower trim (B) by pulling it up. Release the bottom of the left center pillar lower trim from the belt guide (C) in the carpet (D).
Right
Left
  1. Remove the front seat belt upper anchor cover (A), and remove the upper anchor bolt (B).
  1. Remove these items:
    • Front door opening seal, as needed
    • Rear door opening seal, as needed
  1. Pull the bottom of the center pillar upper trim (A) back to detach the clips, and lower it to release the upper hooks (B) from the body holes.
  1. Install the trim in the reverse order of removal, and note these items:
    • If the side curtain airbag has deployed, replace the center pillar upper trim with new one.
    • To prevent the side curtain airbags from deploying improperly and possibly causing injury, inspect removed pieces and replace them if they have any of these types of damage:
      • -
      Any cracks or deformations in the center pillar upper trim (A) and the upper hooks (B), and any stress-whitenings in the upper part of the trim
      -
      Any cracks or deformations in the center pillar lower trim (C), and any breakages in the part (D) that fits into the center pillar upper trim
      -
      Any cracks or stress-whitenings in the clip seating surface (E)
    • Replace the clip (F) if it's damage.
    • Make sure the top of the trim overlaps with the headliner correctly.
    • Make sure the trim hooks are installed into holes in the body securely.
    • Push the clip into place securely.
    • Apply liquid thread lock to the upper anchor bolt before reinstallation.
    • Before installing the anchor bolt, make sure there are no twists or kinks in the front seat belt.
