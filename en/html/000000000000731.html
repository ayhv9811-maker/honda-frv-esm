DTC Troubleshooting: P0500 (17)
DTC P0500:
 VSS Circuit Malfunction
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS.
  1. Test-drive under these conditions:
    • Transmission in 3rd gear
    • Engine speed at 2,000−4,000 rpm (min −1 )
    • Drive for several minutes, then decelerate (with the throttle fully closed) for 5 seconds
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P0500 indicated?
YES -
Intermittent failure, system is OK at this time. Check for poor connections or loose terminals at the VSS and the ECM.■
NO -
Go to Step 5 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the VSS 3P connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure voltage between VSS 3P connector terminal No. 2 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 11 .
NO -
Go to Step 9 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Check the No. 8 IG1 ACG (7.5 A) fuse in under-dash fuse/relay box.
Is the fuse blown?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between the VSS and the No. 8 IG1 ACG (7.5 A) fuse, and replace the No. 8 IG1 ACG (7.5 A) fuse, then go to Step 22 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the VSS and the No. 8 IG1 ACG (7.5 A) fuse, then go to Step 22 .
  1. Measure voltage between VSS 3P connector terminal No. 3 and body ground.
Is there about 5 V?
YES -
Go to Step 19 .
NO -
Go to Step 12 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect ECM connector A (31P).
  1. Check for continuity between VSS 3P connector terminal No. 3 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 15 .
NO -
Go to Step 17 .
  1. Disconnect gauge assembly 20P connector.
  1. Check for continuity between VSS 3P connector terminal No. 3 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between the ECM (A18) and the gauge assembly, the VSS, then go to Step 22 .
NO -
Replace the gauge assembly, then go to Step 22 .
  1. Connect VSS 3P connector terminal No. 3 to body ground with a jumper wire.
  1. Check for continuity between ECM connector terminal A18 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 27 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the ECM (A18) and the VSS, then go to Step 22 .
  1. Check for continuity between VSS 3P connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 20 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the VSS and the G101, then go to Step 22 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Replace the VSS.
  1. Reconnect all connectors.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Retest the ECM with the HDS.
  1. Do the ECM idle learn procedure.
  1. Test-drive under these conditions:
    • Transmission in 3rd gear
    • Engine speed at 2,000−4,000 rpm (min −1 )
    • Drive for several minutes, then decelerate (with the throttle fully closed) for 5 seconds
  1. Check for temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Are any Temporary DTCs or DTCs indicated?
YES -
If DTC P0500 is indicated, check for poor connections or loose terminals at the VSS and the ECM, then go to Step 1 . If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.
NO -
Troubleshooting is complete.■
  1. Update the ECM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good ECM.
  1. Test-drive under these conditions:
    • Transmission in 3rd gear
    • Engine speed at 2,000−4,000 rpm (min −1 )
    • Drive for several minutes, then decelerate (with the throttle fully closed) for 5 seconds
  1. Check for temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Are any Temporary DTCs or DTCs indicated?
YES -
If DTC P0500 is indicated, check for poor connections or loose terminals at the VSS and the ECM, then go to Step 1 . If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.
NO -
If the ECM was updated, troubleshooting is complete. If the ECM was substituted, replace the original ECM. ■
