Interior Trim Removal/Installation - Rear Pillar
Rear Pillar
  1. Fold the rear side seat forward.
  1. Pull the edge of the carpet (A) out from under the trunk side trim panel (B), then remove the rear side seat belt lower anchor bolt (C).
  1. Remove the bracket cover (A) by releasing the hooks (B).
  1. Remove the rear side seat belt upper anchor bolt (A).
  1. Release the hooks (A), then remove the lid (B) from the rear pillar trim (C).
  1. Remove these items:
    • Rear door opening seal, as needed
    • Tailgate weatherstrip, as needed
  1. Release the upper portion of the trunk side trim panel, as needed.
  1. Remove the rear pillar trim (A).
    -1.
    Detach the clips by pulling the trim back.
    -2.
    Lower the trim to release the upper hooks (B) from the body holes.
    -3.
    Pull the rear side seat belt (C) out through a slit (D) in the trim, then pass the lower anchor plate through the opening in the trim.
  1. Install the trim in the reverse order of removal, and note these items:
    • If the side curtain airbag has deployed, replace the rear pillar trim with new one.
    • To prevent the side curtain airbags from deploying improperly and possibly causing injury, inspect removed pieces and replace them if they have any of these types of damage:
      • -
      Any cracks, or deformations in the rear pillar trim (A) and the upper hooks, and any stress-whitenings in the upper part of the trim
      -
      Any cracks or stress-whitenings in the clip seating surfaces (B)
    • Repalce any damage clips (C).
    • Make sure the top of the trim overlaps with the headliner correctly.
    • Make sure the trim hooks are installed into holes in the body securely.
    • Push the clips into place securely.
    • Before installing the anchor bolts, make sure there are no twists or kinks in the front seat belt.
