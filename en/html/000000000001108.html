Water Passage Replacement
  1. Drain the engine coolant.
  1. Remove the alternator.
  1. Remove the A/C condenser fan shroud assembly.
  1. Remove the splash shield.
  1. Remove the A/C compressor without disconnecting the A/C hoses.
  1. Remove the intake manifold.
  1. Remove the harness holder (A) from the bracket, then remove the two bolts (B) securing the connecting pipe.
  1. Remove the lower radiator hose (A), connecting pipe (B), water bypass hose (C), and harness clamp (D).
  1. Remove the positive crankcase ventilation (PCV) hose (A), then remove the water passage (B).
  1. Remove the water pump.
  1. Remove the thermostat housing (A).
  1. Install the thermostat housing with a new O-ring (B).
  1. Install the water pump.
  1. Clean and dry the water passage mating surfaces.
  1. Apply liquid gasket, P/N 08C70-K0234M, 08C70-K0334M, or 08C70-X0331S, evenly to the engine block mating surface of the water passage and to the inner threads of the bolt holes.
    NOTE: Do not install the parts if 4 minutes or more have elapsed since applying liquid gasket. Instead, reapply liquid gasket after removing the old residue.
  1. Install the water passage (A) with a new O-ring (B).
    NOTE:
    • Wait at least 30 minutes before filling the engine with oil.
    • Do not run the engine for at least 3 hours after installing the water passage.
  1. Install the PCV hose (C).
  1. Install the connecting pipe (A) with a new O-ring (B).
  1. Install the lower radiator hose (C), water bypass hose (D), and harness clamp (E).
  1. Tighten the bolts (A) securing the connecting pipe, then install the harness holder (B) to the bracket.
  1. Install the intake manifold.
  1. Install the A/C compressor.
  1. Install the splash shield.
  1. Install the A/C condenser fan shroud assembly.
  1. Install the alternator.
  1. Refill the radiator with engine coolant, and bleed air from the cooling system with the heater valve open.
