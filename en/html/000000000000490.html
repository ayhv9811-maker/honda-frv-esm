DTC Troubleshooting: N
DTC indicator N:
 A Problem in the Blower Motor Circuit
  1. Clear the DTC by turning the ignition switch OFF, and then ON (II).
  1. Operate the climate control system in several modes.
  1. Check for DTCs using the self-diagnostic.
Is DTC N indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 4 .
NO -
Intermittent failure, check for loose wires or poor connections on the blower motor circuit.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Check the No. 29 (40 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box, and the No. 18 (7.5 A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box.
Are the fuses OK?
YES -
Go to Step 6 .
NO -
Replace the fuse(s), and recheck.■
  1. Connect the No. 2 terminal of the blower motor 2P connector to body ground with a jumper wire.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
Does the blower motor run?
YES -
Go to Step 8 .
NO -
Go to Step 24 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the jumper wire.
  1. Disconnect the power transistor 4P connector.
  1. Check for continuity between the No. 3 terminal of the power transistor 4P connector and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 12 .
NO -
Check for an open in the BLK wire between the power transistor and body ground. If the wire is OK, check for poor ground at G503.■
  1. Connect the No. 1 and No. 3 terminals of the power transistor 4P connector with a jumper wire.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
Does the blower motor run at high speed?
YES -
Go to Step 14 .
NO -
Repair open in the YEL wire between the power transistor and the blower motor.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the jumper wire.
  1. Disconnect climate control unit connector A (16P).
  1. Check for continuity between the No. 1 and No. 2 terminals of climate control unit connector A (16P) and body ground individually.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair any short to body ground in the wire(s) between the climate control unit and the power transistor.■
NO -
Go to Step 18 .
  1. Check for continuity between the following terminals of climate control unit connector A (16P) and the power transistor 4P connector.

  1. Check for voltage between the No. 1 and No. 2 terminals of climate control unit connector A (16P) and body ground individually.

YES -
Repair short to power in the wires.■

  1. Test the power transistor.

Is there battery voltage?
YES -

NO -

  1. Remove the blower motor relay from the under-hood fuse/relay box, and test it.

NO -

  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between the No. 3 terminal of the blower motor relay 4P socket and body ground.

YES -
Go to Step 32 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the No. 18 fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box and the blower motor relay.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Check for continuity between the No. 4 terminal of the blower motor relay 4P socket and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair open in the GRN wire between the blower motor relay and the blower motor.■
NO -
Check for an open in the wire between the blower motor relay and body ground. If the wire is OK, check for poor ground at G201 (D17A engine model) or G301 (K20A engine model).■
