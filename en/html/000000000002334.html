Clutch Hose Replacement
NOTE:
  • Use fender covers to avoid damaging painted surfaces.
  • Do not spill brake fluid on the vehicle; it may damage the paint; if brake fluid does contact the paint, wash it off immediately with water.
  1. Make sure you have the anti-theft codes for the radio and navigation system, then write down the frequencies for the radio presets.
  1. Disconnect the negative (âˆ’) cable first, then the positive (+) cable from the battery. Remove the battery.
  1. Remove the air cleaner housing.
  1. Disconnect the clutch line (A) from the clutch hose (B) using a 10 mm flare nut wrench.
  1. Remove the hose clips (A), and the clutch hose (B).
  1. Connect the clutch line (A), and the clutch hose (B) is hand-tight.
  1. Install the clips (A), and tighten the clutch line (B).
  1. Install the air cleaner housing.
  1. Install the battery, then connect the battery cables.
  1. Bleed the clutch hydraulic system.
