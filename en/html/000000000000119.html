Heater Unit/Core Replacement
SRS components are located in this area. Review the SRS component locations, and the precautions and procedures before performing repairs or service.
NOTE: RHD type is shown, LHD type is symmetrical.
  1. Make sure you have the anti-theft codes for the radio and the navigation system, then write down the frequencies for the radio's preset buttons.
  1. Make sure the ignition is OFF, then disconnect the negative cable from the battery.
  1. Disconnect the suction and receiver lines from the evaporator core.
  1. From under the hood, open the cable clamp (A), then disconnect the heater valve cable (B) from the heater valve arm (C). Turn the heater valve arm to the fully opened position as shown.
  1. When the engine is cool, drain the engine coolant from the radiator, D17A2 engine model, K20A9 engine model.
  1. Slide the hose clamps (A) back. Remove the unit and water valve bracket, then disconnect the inlet heater hose (B) and the outlet heater hose (C) from the heater unit. Engine coolant will run out when the hoses are disconnected; drain it into a clean drip pan. Be sure not to let coolant spill on the electrical parts or the painted surfaces. If any coolant spills, rinse it off immediately.
  1. Remove the mounting nut from the heater unit. Take care not to damage or bend the fuel lines and the brake lines, etc.
  1. Remove the dashboard.
  1. Remove the ECM, D17A2 engine model, K20A9 engine model.
  1. Disconnect the connector (A) from the blower motor.
  1. Disconnect the connector (A) from the air mix control motor, then remove the wire harness clips (B).
  1. Disconnect the connectors (A) from the evaporator temperature sensor, the mode control motor, and the recirculation control motor, then remove the wire harness clip (B) and the wire harness (C).
  1. Remove the mounting nuts, then remove the blower heater unit (A).
  1. Remove the self-tapping screws, then remove the heater core cover (A). Remove the grommet (B) and carefully pull out the heater core (C) so you don't bend the inlet and outlet pipes.
  1. Install the heater core and the evaporator core in the reverse order of removal.
  1. Install the heater unit in the reverse order of removal, and note these items:
    • Do not interchange the inlet and outlet heater hoses, and install the hose clamps securely.
    • Refill the cooling system with engine coolant, D17A2 engine model, K20A9 engine model.
    • Adjust the heater valve cable.
    • Make sure that there is no coolant leakage.
    • Make sure that there is no air leakage.
    • Refer to evaporator core replacement.
    • Enter the anti-theft codes for the radio and the navigation system, then enter the customer's radio station presets.
