Cylinder Head Removal
NOTE:
  • Use fender covers to avoid damaging painted surfaces.
  • To avoid damage, unplug the wiring connectors carefully while holding the connector portion.
  • To avoid damaging the cylinder head, wait until the engine coolant temperature drops below 38 °C (100 °F) before loosening the cylinder head bolts.
  • Mark all wiring and hoses to avoid misconnection. Also, be sure that they do not contact other wiring or hoses, or interfere with other parts.
  1. Relieve fuel pressure.
  1. Drain the engine coolant.
  1. Disconnect the negative cable from the battery first, then disconnect the positive cable. Remove the battery.
  1. Remove the harness clamp (A), then remove the resonator (B).
  1. Remove the air cleaner housing.
  1. Remove the quick-connect fitting cover (A), then disconnect the fuel feed hose.
  1. Remove the evaporative emission (EVAP) canister hose (B).
  1. Remove the ground cables (A), harness clamp (B), and harness clamp bracket mounting bolt (C).
  1. Remove the upper radiator hose (A), lower radiator hose (B), heater hose (C), and connecting pipe (D).
  1. Remove the engine wire harness connectors and wire harness clamps from the cylinder head.
    • Four injector connectors
    • Manifold absolute pressure (MAP) sensor connector
    • Engine coolant temperature (ECT) sensor connector
    • Throttle position sensor connector
    • Idle air control (IAC) valve connector
    • Camshaft position (CMP) sensor connector
    • VTEC solenoid valve connector
    • Engine oil pressure (EOP) sensor connector
  1. Remove the intake manifold.
  1. Remove the exhaust manifold.
  1. Remove the timing belt.
  1. Remove the cylinder head bolts. To prevent warpage, unscrew the bolts in sequence 1/3 turn at a time; repeat the sequence until all bolts are loosened.
  1. Remove the cylinder head.
