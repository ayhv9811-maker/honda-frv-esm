Alternator FR Signal Circuit Troubleshooting
  1. Start the engine, and let it idle.
  1. Monitor the ALTERNATOR in the DATA LIST with the HDS.
  1. Check if the indicated percentage varies when the headlight switch is on.
Does the percentage vary?
YES -
The alternator signal circuit is OK.■
NO -
Go to Step 4 .
  1. Turn the headlight switch and ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the alternator 4P connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure voltage between ECM connector terminals A9 and B13.
Is there about 5 V?
YES -
Go to Step 8 .
NO -
Go to Step 12 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect ECM connector B (24P).
  1. Connect alternator 4P connector terminal No. 4 to body ground with a jumper wire.
  1. Check for continuity between body ground and ECM connector terminal B13.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Test the alternator. ■
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the ECM (B13) and the alternator.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect ECM connector B (24P).
  1. Check for continuity between body ground and ECM connector terminal B13.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between the ECM (B13) and the alternator.■
NO -
Update the ECM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good ECM, then recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away with a known-good ECM, replace the original ECM .■
