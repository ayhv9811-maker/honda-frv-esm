Special Tools Required

Belt Tension Gauge Method (D17A2 Engine Model)
Inspection
  1. Remove the P/S reservoir from the bracket, and set it aside.
  1. Attach the belt tension gauge to the belt, and measure the tension of the belt. Follow the gauge manufacturer's instructions. If the belt is worn or damaged, replace it.
    NOTE: Remove the belt tension gauge carefully to avoid hitting the gauge reset lever.
    Tension:
    Used belt:
    390−540 N (40−55 kgf, 88−121 lbf)
    New belt:
    740−880 N (75−90 kgf, 165−198 lbf)

  1. If you installed a new belt, run the engine for 5 minutes, then readjust the belt to the used belt specification.
Adjustment
  1. Loosen the power steering pump mounting nut (A) and pump locknut (B).
  1. Turn the adjusting bolt (C) to get the proper belt tension, then retighten the mounting nut and locknut.
  1. Start the engine and turn the steering wheel from lock-to-lock several times, then stop the engine and recheck the tension of the belt.
Deflection Method (D17A2 Engine Model)
Inspection
  1. Apply a force of 98 N (10 kgf, 22 lbf) and measure the deflection between the power steering pump pulley (A) and the crankshaft pulley (B). If the belt is worn or damaged, replace it.
    Deflection:
    Used belt:
    13.0−16.5 mm (0.51−0.65 in.)
    New belt:
    9.0−11.0 mm (0.35−0.43 in.)

  1. If you installed a new belt, run the engine for 5 minutes, then readjust the belt to the used belt specification.
Adjustment
  1. Loosen the power steering pump mounting nut (A) and pump locknut (B).
  1. Turn the adjusting bolt (C) to get the proper belt tension, then retighten the mounting nut and locknut.
  1. Start the engine and turn the steering wheel from lock-to-lock several times, then stop the engine and recheck the tension of the belt.
