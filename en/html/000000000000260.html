Dashboard Replacement
SRS components are located in this area. Review the SRS component locations and the precautions and procedures before performing repairs or service.
NOTE:
  • When prying components, use the handy remover set or equivalent.
  • Have an assistant help you when removing and installing the dashboard.
  • Take care not to scratch the dashboard, body and other related parts.
  • Put on gloves to protect your hands.
  • RHD is shown; LHD is symmetrical.
  1. Disconnect the negative battery cable, and wait at least 3 minutes before beginning work.
  1. Remove these items:
    • Driver's dashboard trim
    • Driver's under cover
    • Driver's dashboard lower cover
    • Driver's middle panel
    • Beverage holder
    • Passenger's dashboard trim
    • Glove box
    • Kick panels, both sides
    • Front pillar trim, both sides
    • Steering column
    • Gauge control module
    • Audio unit, without navigation system
    • Display panel control unit, with navigation system
    • GPS antenna, with navigation system
  1. Disconnect the power window switch connector (A).
  1. Disconnect the in-car temperature sensor connector (A) and air hose (B).
  1. Remove the screws, disconnect the glove box light connector (A), and detach the harness clips (B), then remove the glove box striker (C).
  1. Remove the passenger's airbag mounting bolts.
  1. Remove the screws securing the driver's side and the center part of the dashboard (A).
  1. Remove the bolts (A) and screws (B) securing the passenger's side of the dashboard (C).
  1. Wrap the change lever with a shop towel to prevent damage.
  1. Lift up on the dashboard (A) to disengage the hooks (B) on the passenger's airbag from a bracket (C) on the steering hanger beam, then hold the dashboard.
  1. Disconnect the passenger's airbag connector (D) and sunlight sensor connector (E), then detach the connector clip (F).
  1. Lift up on the dashboard (A) to release it from the guide pins (B), then remove it.
  1. Install the dashboard in the reverse order of removal, and note these items:
    • Make sure the dashboard wire harness is not pinched.
    • Make sure the connectors are plugged in properly, and the air hose is connected securely.
