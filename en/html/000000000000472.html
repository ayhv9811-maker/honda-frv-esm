DTC Troubleshooting: 54
DTC 54:
 ABS Fail-safe Relay
NOTE: If the DTCs shown following indicated with the DTC 54 at a time, troubleshoot DTCs shown following first, then troubleshoot the DTC 54.
DTC 31, 32, 33, 34, 35, 36, 37, 38: ABS Solenoid
  1. Disconnect the ABS control unit 26P connector.
  1. Check the No. 15 (20 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box.
Is the fuse OK?
YES -
Reinstall the fuse, then go to Step 3 .
NO -
Go to Step 5 .
  1. Mesure the voltage between ABS control unit 26P connector terminal No. 24 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 4 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between No. 15 (20 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box and ABS modulator-control unit.■
  1. Check for continuity between ABS control unit 26P connector terminal No. 23 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between ABS modulator-control unit and body ground (G303).■
  1. Check for continuity between ABS control unit 26P connector terminal No. 24 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short to body ground in the wire between No. 15 (20 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box and the ABS modulator-control unit.■
NO -
Go to Step 6 .
  1. Reconnect the ABS control unit 26P connector.
  1. Clear the DTCs.
  1. Test-drive the vehicle at 15 km/h (9 mph) or more.
  1. Check the DTCs.
Does the ABS indicator come on, and are DTC 54 indicated?
YES -
Check for loose or poor connections in the ABS control unit 26P connector. If connections are good, replace the ABS modulator-control unit .■
NO -
The system is OK at this time.■
