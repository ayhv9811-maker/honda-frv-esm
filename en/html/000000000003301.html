Cam Chain Removal
NOTE: Keep the cam chain away from magnetic fields.
  1. Remove the front wheels.
  1. Remove the splash shield.
  1. Remove the drive belt auto-tensioner.
  1. Remove the cylinder head cover.
  1. Set the No. 1 piston at top dead center (TDC). The ‘‘UP'' mark (A) on the camshaft sprocket should be at the top, and the TDC grooves (B) on the camshaft sprocket should line up with the top edge of the head.
  1. Remove the positive crankcase ventilation (PCV) hose.
  1. Remove the crankshaft pulley.
  1. Support the engine with a jack and a wood block under the oil pan.
  1. Remove the upper torque rod (B).
M/T
A/T
  1. Remove the ground cable (A), then remove the side engine mount bracket (B).
  1. Remove the oil pump.
  1. Measure the tensioner rod length between the tensioner body and bottom of the flat surface section on the tensioner rod. If the length is more than the tolerance, replace the cam chain.
    Tensioner Rod Length
    Service Limit:
    14.5 mm (0.57 in.)

  1. Loosely install the crankshaft pulley.
  1. Turn the crankshaft counterclockwise to compress the auto-tensioner.
  1. Align the holes on the lock (A) and the auto-tensioner (B), then insert a 1.0 mm (0.04 in.) diameter pin (C) into the holes. Turn the crankshaft clockwise to secure the pin.
  1. Remove the auto-tensioner.
  1. Remove the crankshaft pulley.
  1. Remove the cam chain guide (A) and cam chain tensioner arm (B).
  1. Remove the cam chain.
