Audio System Symptom Troubleshooting - Radio station has poor reception or can be received
Radio station has poor reception or can not be received
NOTE: Check the radio reception in an open area. Poor reception/interference can be caused by the following:
  • The radio station is far away.
  • A tall building, a mountain, or high-voltage power line is nearby.
  1. Check if each radio station can be received normally. Compare the radio reception/interference to another vehicle of the same model and trim level in the exact same conditions.
Are they the same?
YES -
Operation is normal.■
NO -
Go to Step 2 .
  1. Disconnect and check the antenna lead for loose or a poor connection.
Reconnect the antenna lead and recheck the symptom; does it still appear?
YES -
Go to Step 3 .
NO -
Operation is normal.■
  1. Check for continuity between the window antenna terminal (A), and the antenna cable center terminal at the audio unit lead.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 4 .
NO -
Replace the antenna cable.■
  1. Check for continuity between the antenna cable center terminal at the audio unit lead, and the antenna cable outer terminal at the audio unit lead.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Replace the antenna cable.■
NO -
Go to Step 5 .
  1. Measure the voltage between the antenna cable terminal (A) at the audio unit lead and body ground.
Is there 12 V?
YES -
Faulty audio unit.■
NO -
