Note these items during removal:
  • Using solvent and a brush, wash any oil and dirt off the valve body, its lines, and the end of the gearbox. Blow dry with compressed air.
  • Make sure to remove the steering wheel before disconnecting the steering joint. Damage to the cable reel can occur.
  • The illustration show RHD type, LHD type is same procedure.
  • Remove and install the steering gearbox bracket as necessary.
  • Take care not to spill the fluid on the body or parts. Wipe off any spilled fluid at once.
  1. Raise the front of vehicle, and make sure it is securely supported.
  1. Remove the front wheels.
  1. Remove the driver's airbag and the steering wheel.
  1. Disconnect the steering joint (A) and pinion shaft (B).
  1. Remove the center guide (C). Discard the center guide after removing it. The center guide might not be equipped in a car for some reasons such as by removing the steering gearbox.
  1. Remove the cotter pin (A) from the joint part of the tie-rod end (B) ball joint, and loosen the nut.
  1. Separate the tie-rod ball joint and damper steering arm using the boll joint remover.
  1. Remove the return pipe clamp (A).
  1. Remove the feed pipe bracket (A) and return pipe clamp (B).
Front passenger's seat side
Driver's seat side
  1. Loosen the flare nuts (A). Disconnect the feed pipe (B) and return pipe (C) from the valve body unit and plug them.
  1. Remove the gearbox mounting bracket (A) and cushion (B) from the front passenger's seat side of the steering gearbox.
  1. Remove the gearbox mounting bracket (A) from the driver's seat side of the steering gearbox.
  1. Turn the gearbox to bring the pinion shaft (A) toward the front of the car, and remove the pinion shaft grommet (B).
  1. Carefully move the steering gearbox (A) and tie-rods (B) as an assembly toward the driver's side until the pinion shaft clears the heater hoses. Remove the steering gearbox through the wheel well opening on the driver's side.
  1. Remove the steering gearbox bracket (A).
  1. After removing the steering gearbox, make sure that no power steering fluid gets on the gearbox mount cushions, gearbox housing, and the surface of the front subframe. Wipe off any spilled fluid at once.
