VSA System Indicator Circuit Troubleshooting
VSA indicator does not come on
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II), and watch the VSA indicator.
Does the VSA indicator come on and then go off?
YES -
The system is OK at this time.■
NO -
Go to Step 2 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the VSA control unit 46P connector.
  1. Remove the gauge assembly.
  1. Disconnect the gauge assembly connector B (28P).
  1. Check for continuity between gauge assembly connector B (28P) terminal No. 26 and the body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short to body ground in the wire between the gauge assembly and the VSA modulator-control unit.■
NO -
Go to Step 7 .
  1. Connect the gauge assembly connector A (20P).
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II), and watch the VSA indicator.
Does the VSA indicator come on ?
YES -
Replace the VSA modulator-control unit. ■
NO -
Replace the gauge assembly. ■
VSA indicator does not go off, and no DTCs are stored
  1. Check the No. 3 (7.5 A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box.
Is the fuse OK?
YES -
Go to Step 2 .
NO -
Replace the fuse, and recheck.■
  1. Disconnect the VSA control unit 46P connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between the VSA control unit 46P connector terminal No. 40 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the No. 3 (7.5 A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box and the VSA modulator-control unit.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Check for continuity between the VSA control unit 46P connector terminal No. 43 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 7 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the VSA modulator-control unit and body ground (G204).■
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Connect the VSA control unit 46P connector terminal No. 9 and body ground with a jumper wire.
Does the VSA indicator go off?
YES -
Check for loose or poor connections in the VSA control unit 46P connector. If connections are good, replace the VSA modulator-control unit. ■
NO -
Go to Step 9 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Remove the gauge assembly, and leave the connectors connected.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Connect the gauge assembly connector B (28P) terminal No. 26 and body ground with a jumper wire.
Does the VSA indicator go off?
YES -
Repair open in the wire between the gauge assembly and the VSA modulator-control unit.■
NO -
Check for loose or poor connections in the gauge assembly connectors. If connections are good, replace the gauge assembly. ■
