Rear Damper/Spring Replacement

Exploded View
Removal
  1. Raise the rear of the vehicle, and support it with safety stands in the proper locations.
  1. Remove the rear wheel.
  1. Remove the flange nut (A), while holding the joint pin (B) with a hex wrench (C), and disconnect the stabilizer link (D) from the trailing arm.
  1. Remove the flange bolt (E), and disconnect the damper (F) from the trailing arm.
  1. Remove the lid on the trunk side trim.
  1. Remove the flange nuts from the top of the damper in the cargo area.
  1. Remove the trailing arm front mounting bolts.
  1. Lower the rear suspension, then remove the damper (A) from the vehicle.
Disassembly/Inspection
  1. Compress the damper spring with a commercially available strut spring compressor (A) according to the manufacturer's instructions, then remove the self-locking nut (B) while holding the damper shaft with a hex wrench (C). Do not compress the spring more than necessary to remove the nut.
  1. Release the pressure from the strut spring compressor, then disassemble the damper as shown in the Exploded View.
  1. Reassemble all the parts, except for the spring mounting cushion and spring.
  1. Compress the damper assembly by hand, and check for smooth operation through a full stroke, both compression and extension. The damper should extend smoothly and constantly when compression is released. If it does not, the gas is leaking and the damper should be replaced.
  1. Check for oil leaks, abnormal noises, and binding during these tests.
Reassembly
  1. Install all parts except the damper mounting washer and self-locking nut onto the damper unit by referring to the Exploded View. Align the bottom of the spring (A) and the stepped part of the lower spring seat (B), and align the damper mounting base (C) as shown.
  1. Install the damper assembly on a commercially available strut spring compressor.
  1. Compress the damper spring with the strut spring compressor.

Right side:
  1. Install the washer (A) and a new self-locking nut (B) on the damper shaft.
  1. Hold the damper shaft with a hex wrench (C), and tighten the self-locking nut to the specified torque value.
Installation
  1. Position the damper assembly (A) in the body. Note the direction of the damper mounting base so that the small hole dot (B) on it is toward the inside of the vehicle.
  1. Loosely install the flange nuts (A) onto the top of the damper.
  1. Loosely install the trailing arm front mounting bolts (A).
  1. Loosely install the flange bolt (A) on the bottom of the damper (B).
  1. Connect the stabilizer link (C) to the trailing arm (D), and loosely install the flange nut (E), while holding the joint pin (F) with a hex wrench (G).
  1. Raise the rear suspension with a floor jack to load the vehicle weight, and tighten the nuts and bolts to the specified torque values.
  1. Clean the mating surface of the brake disc/drum and the inside of the wheel, then install the rear wheel.
  1. Check the rear wheel alignment, and adjust it if necessary.
