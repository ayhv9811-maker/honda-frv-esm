Engine Removal
Special Tools Required

Sub hanger stay
 07MAK-PY30100

Engine tilt hanger set
 07KAK-SJ40101

Engine hanger stay
 07KAK-SJ40200
These tools use only for except EU models.
NOTE:
  • Use fender covers to avoid damaging painted surfaces.
  • To avoid damaging the wiring and terminals, unplug the wiring connectors carefully while holding the connector portion.
  • Mark all wiring and hoses to avoid misconnection. Also, be sure that they do not contact other wiring or hoses, or interfere with other parts.
  1. Remove the washer hose (A) from the clamp (B).
  1. Secure the hood in the wide open position (support rod in the lower hole).
  1. Relieve fuel pressure.
  1. Disconnect the negative cable from the battery, then disconnect the positive cable.
  1. Remove the battery.
  1. Remove the air cleaner housing assembly.
  1. Remove the harness clamp (A), then remove the air intake duct (B).
  1. Remove the three bolts (A), and loosen the two bolts (B), then remove the battery base (C).
  1. Remove the injector cover.
  1. Remove the quick-connect fitting cover (A), then disconnect the fuel feed hose.
  1. Remove the evaporative emission (EVAP) canister hose (A), brake booster vacuum hose (B), and power steering (P/S) hose clamp (C).
  1. Remove the battery cables (A) from the under-hood fuse/relay box.
  1. Remove the harness clamp (B).
  1. Remove the engine control module (ECM)/powertrain control module (PCM) cover (A), then remove the three bolts (B) securing the ECM/PCM.
  1. Disconnect the ECM/PCM connectors (A) and engine wire harness connectors (B).
  1. Remove the harness clamp (C).
  1. Remove the ECM/PCM bracket.
  1. Remove the shift cable (M/T).
  1. Remove the clutch slave cylinder and clutch line bracket mounting bolt (M/T).
  1. Remove the drive belt.
  1. Remove the radiator cap.
  1. Raise the vehicle on the lift to full height.
  1. Remove the front wheels.
  1. Remove the splash shield.
  1. Loosen the drain plug in the radiator, and drain the engine coolant.
  1. Drain the engine oil.
  1. Drain the transmission fluid:
    • M/T
    • A/T
  1. Remove exhaust pipe A.
  1. Remove the shift cable (A/T).
  1. Separate the stabilizer links.
  1. Separate the knuckles from the lower arms.
  1. Remove the driveshafts. Coat all precision-finished surfaces with clean engine oil. Tie plastic bags over the driveshaft ends.
  1. Remove the P/S pump without disconnecting the P/S hoses from the pump.
  1. Lower the vehicle on the lift.
  1. Remove the radiator.
  1. Disconnect the A/C compressor clutch connector (A), then remove the harness clamps (B).
  1. Remove the A/C compressor (C) without disconnecting the A/C hoses.
  1. Remove the heater hoses.
  1. Remove the automatic transmission fluid (ATF) cooler hose from the transmission, then plug the line and hose (A/T).
  1. Raise the vehicle on the lift to full height.
  1. Remove the lower torque rod.
  1. Lower the vehicle on the lift.

  1. Attach the special tools (Commercially available tool for EU models) to the engine as shown.
  1. Remove the upper torque rod.

A/T
  1. Remove the ground cable (A), then remove the side engine mount bracket (B).
  1. Remove the ground cable (A), then remove the transmission mount (B).
  1. Check that the engine/transmission is completely free of vacuum hoses, fuel and coolant hoses, and electrical wiring.
  1. Slowly raise the engine/transmission assembly about 150 mm (6 in.). Check once again that all hoses and electrical wiring are disconnected and free from the engine/transmission.
  1. Raise the engine all the way, and remove it from the vehicle.
