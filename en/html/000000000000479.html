DTC Troubleshooting: C
DTC indicator C:
 An Open in the Outside Air Temperature Sensor Circuit
  1. Clear the DTC by turning the ignition switch OFF, and then ON (II).
  1. Operate the climate control system in several modes.
  1. Check for DTCs using the self-diagnostic.
Is DTC C indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 4 .
NO -
Intermittent failure, check for loose wires or poor connections on the outside air temperature sensor circuit.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Remove the outside air temperature sensor and test it.
Does the outside air temperature sensor test OK?
YES -
Go to Step 6 .
NO -
Replace the outside air temperature sensor .■
  1. Disconnect climate control unit connector B (20P).
  1. Check for continuity between the No. 14 terminal of climate control unit connector B (20P) and the No. 2 terminal of the outside air temperature sensor 2P connector.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 8 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the climate control unit and the outside air temperature sensor.■
  1. Check for continuity between the No. 15 terminal of climate control unit connector B (20P) and the No. 1 terminal of the outside air temperature sensor 2P connector.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 9 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the climate control unit and the outside air temperature sensor.■
  1. Substitute a known-good climate control unit, and recheck.
Is the symptom/indication goes away?
YES -
Replace the original climate control unit .■
NO -
Replace the climate control panel.■
